Understanding The Dice

ALIEN uses the Year Zero Engine. When you attempt something risky, roll a pool of d6s equal to Attribute + Skill. Each 6 rolled is a success. Most tasks require one success.

D
Base Dice Yellow d6s. Roll Attribute + Skill dice. Sixes succeed. More dice = better chance.
G
Gear Dice Equipment grants bonus d6s. Good gear helps. Broken gear doesn't.
S
Stress Dice Black d6s. Add dice = higher chance to succeed. But ones on Stress dice trigger Panic.
!
Pushing Failed? Push it once. Reroll all non-sixes. Ones on Base dice = damage or broken gear.
‚ö† STRESS WARNING Stress makes you better ‚Äî and more fragile. Every Stress die you add increases your chance of success, but rolling a 1 on any Stress die triggers a Panic Roll. Panic can cause you to freeze, flee, or worse. Stress accumulates. It doesn't go away easily. The longer you're in danger, the closer you are to breaking.

At Stress 10, you are BROKEN ‚Äî incapacitated by terror until you can recover. Stress is removed slowly: one point per Turn of rest in a safe location, or through specific Talents and medical intervention.

Panic Roll

When a 1 appears on any Stress die, immediately roll 1d6 + current Stress Level and consult the Panic Table. Results range from temporary hesitation (6 or less) to complete psychological breakdown, fleeing in terror, or attacking allies (13+). The higher your Stress, the worse the Panic.

Health & Damage

Your Health equals your Strength Attribute. When you take damage, mark it off your Health track. At 0 Health, you are Broken and cannot act without help. At negative Health equal to your Strength, you die.

Critical Injuries

When damage reduces you to 0 Health or you take damage equal to or greater than your Strength in one hit, roll on the Critical Injury table. Critical Injuries produce specific wounds with mechanical and narrative consequences: broken limbs, internal bleeding, collapsed lung ‚Äî each with time limits, penalties, and treatment requirements.

‚ö† MEDICAL NOTICE Critical Injuries require treatment within a time limit or they worsen. Some require surgery. Untreated Critical Injuries can kill you even after the immediate threat is over. Space is not forgiving. Neither is biology.

Personal Agenda

Every character has a Personal Agenda ‚Äî a hidden goal, secret directive, or private motivation that may conflict with the mission or the crew. Work with the GM to establish your Agenda. It should create tension and difficult choices.

Signature Item

Choose one Signature Item ‚Äî a personal possession that defines your character. It has no mechanical benefit, but it matters to you. Losing it is a narrative crisis. Examples: family photo, military service medal, lucky charm, last letter from someone who didn't make it, corporate contract you're trying to pay off.

The Signature Item is a GM tool for personal stakes. It can be threatened, stolen, destroyed ‚Äî forcing you to choose between pragmatism and what you care about.

  • Rival: Someone you clash with. What's the tension?
  • Dependent: Someone who relies on you ‚Äî or you on them.

Mission Briefing

Work with the GM to understand the mission parameters:

  • What ship are you on? What's its designation and current status?
  • What's the official mission? Cargo haul? Survey? Colony support?
  • How long until you reach your destination?
  • What are the standing orders if something goes wrong?

Space is hostile. The Company is indifferent. Your crew is all you have ‚Äî and even they may not be trustworthy when it comes down to survival versus the mission. Stay alert. Stay alive. Stay human. But understand: when the choice comes between those three things, you may only get to keep one.

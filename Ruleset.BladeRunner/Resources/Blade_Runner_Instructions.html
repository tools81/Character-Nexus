You're a Blade Runner working for the Rep-Detect Unit of the LAPD in a rain-soaked Los Angeles circa 2037. The Tyrell Corporation is gone. Replicants walk the streets — some knowing what they are, others living lies. Your job is to find them. The question that follows you everywhere: what are you?

01 Choose Your Archetype

Your Archetype is your operative role within the Rep-Detect Unit. It defines your starting attributes, key skills, and how you approach the casework and moral complexity of the job.

INSP
Inspector
The classic Blade Runner. Sharp instincts, combat-ready, and carrying the weight of every retirement they've ordered. Balanced stat spread, strong in Coolness and Strength.
ENFC
Enforcer
Brute force and raw intimidation. The Unit's blunt instrument — effective, unsettling, and difficult to reason with when the heat is on.
ANAT
Analyst
The technical mind of the unit. Forensics, data retrieval, replicant biology, and interrogation through clinical detachment rather than aggression.
INFM
Informant
Lives in the city's shadows — the deals, the contacts, the whisper networks. More street than precinct. Information is the currency; everyone owes something.
RPLT
Replicant
You are one of them — working for the unit that hunts your kind. The question of your own humanity is not abstract. It is the central fact of your existence.

Your Archetype provides starting ratings for each attribute, your Key Attribute (used for Pushing rolls), a starting Specialty, and a unique Archetype ability.

03 The Dice System

Blade Runner uses the Year Zero Engine. When your character attempts something with meaningful risk, you build a dice pool and roll all at once looking for successes.

D
Base Dice (d6) Count your Attribute + Skill value — roll that many d6s. Each 6 is a success.
G
Gear Dice (d6) Relevant gear adds extra dice. Gear has a Bonus Die rating. Also d6s — sixes succeed.
S
Stress Dice (d6) Stress adds dice — but ones rolled on stress dice cause Panic. High-risk, high-reward.
!
Pushing Failed roll? Push it — reroll all non-success dice once. Ones on base dice cause damage or Conditions.

Most tasks require one success. Extra successes can be spent on stunts — extra effects, faster resolution, or helping allies. The system is fast and brutal, as the city demands.

06 Humanity

Humanity is the most important number on your sheet — and the hardest to protect. It begins at Empathy × 2 and can be damaged by the moral weight of the work: retiring replicants, watching the city's suffering, moral compromise, and the slow erosion of what you tell yourself you are.

  • High Humanity (8–10): Fully present, emotionally intact — and therefore vulnerable. The city feels everything more sharply.
  • Mid Humanity (4–7): Functional detachment. You do the job. You try not to think about it too much.
  • Low Humanity (1–3): Burnt out, morally hollowed, or something worse. The work has gotten inside you.
  • Humanity 0: You are Broken — not dead, but unable to function as an operative without help. Something fundamental has given way.

Humanity can be recovered — through rest, meaningful connection, or moments of genuine human contact — but the city is not generous with those.

07 Gear & Equipment

Every Blade Runner operative is issued standard LAPD equipment on deployment. Additional gear can be requisitioned, purchased on the street, or acquired through the city's grey markets.

Standard Issue

  • LAPD Blaster (PKD): The iconic sidearm — reliable, lethal at close range, and recognized everywhere. Bonus die for intimidation when visible.
  • Badge & ID: Your authority in a city that barely respects authority. Useful — sometimes.
  • Spinner Access: Unit-assigned aerial vehicle. Requires Driving (Spinners) specialty for full performance.
  • Voigt-Kampff Apparatus: For the Interrogation of suspected replicants. Measures involuntary micro-response. Cumbersome, but irreplaceable.
  • ESPER Terminal: Photo analysis system. The machine sees what the eye can't. Slow but thorough.
  • Comms Device & Database Access: City records, LAPD files, Tyrell Registry (heavily redacted).

Gear Ratings

All equipment has a Gear Bonus (0–3 bonus dice) and a Reliability value. When you Push a roll using gear, roll a reliability die — a 1 means the gear takes damage. Damaged gear adds fewer dice. Destroyed gear adds none.

⚠ BLACK MARKET NOTE Unlicensed weapons, Nexus-series contacts, and off-registry replicant parts are available through the right connections — but possession invites the kind of attention that ends careers and sometimes lives. The city runs on two sets of rules.

08 Relationships & Contacts

No one survives this city alone. Establish at least three Relationships at character creation — these are the people who complicate your existence in ways both useful and dangerous.

// RELATIONSHIP CATEGORIES — SELECT OR CREATE
01 Partner / Colleague: Someone in the unit you work closely with. Trust level? What do you owe each other?
02 Informant / Contact: A street-level source. How did the relationship form? What keeps it going?
03 Personal: Someone outside the work — a family member, a lover, a memory. The thing that reminds you there's something to lose.
04 Antagonist: Someone who complicates your life. A rival in the unit. A target who got away. A person who knows something about you.
05 Unknown: Optional. Someone you're connected to but don't fully understand. The Game Runner will use this.

09 The Question of What You Are

All Blade Runner operatives — including Replicants — answer the following profile questions during character creation. These questions have no mechanical function. They exist to establish who your character believes themselves to be.

Answer them honestly. Or don't. The machine can always tell the difference. Can you?

// VOIGT-KAMPFF PROFILE — OPERATIVE INTAKE
VK1 What is the first memory you are certain is real?
VK2 You've been assigned to retire a subject. You find them in a school. What do you do?
VK3 What do you do on your days off? Do you have days off?
VK4 If you discovered you were a Replicant, what would you do next?
VK5 What do you want? Not what the department wants. Not what the city needs. You.

Your Case

Work with the Game Runner to establish the central case you'll be investigating. The Blade Runner RPG is case-driven — each session is built around an investigation with a defined location, suspects, and escalating complications. Your starting case should connect to at least one of your Relationships.

The Drive

Every operative has something that keeps them walking through the rain. Name it — a person, a truth, a debt. The Game Runner will find ways to threaten it.

The city does not care about your answers. It has never cared. There are four million people between the acid clouds and the flooded streets, and somewhere among them are things that look like people but were made in factories. Your job is to find them. What you do after — what you feel after — that's between you and whatever you decide you are.

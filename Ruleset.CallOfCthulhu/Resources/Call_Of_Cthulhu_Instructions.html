Call of Cthulhu

Horror Roleplaying in the Worlds of H.P. Lovecraft
Seventh Edition — Chaosium Inc.
The most merciful thing in the world, I think, is the inability of the human mind to correlate all its contents. We live on a placid island of ignorance in the midst of black seas of infinity, and it was not meant that we should voyage far. You are about to create an Investigator — someone who will venture into those dark waters and discover truths that shatter sanity.

Calculate Derived Attributes

Several crucial values are derived from your characteristics. These determine your survivability and capacity for action in the face of cosmic horror.

Attribute Formula Function
Hit Points (CON + SIZ) ÷ 10 How much physical damage you can sustain
Magic Points POW ÷ 5 Used to cast spells and resist supernatural influence
Sanity Points POW Mental stability — lost through witnessing the unnatural
Move Rate Based on age, DEX, and STR Movement per combat round — typically 7 to 9
Damage Bonus STR + SIZ combined Extra damage in hand-to-hand combat
Build STR + SIZ combined Resistance to being moved or knocked down

Allocate Skill Points

Skills begin at a base value determined by their governing characteristic. You receive points to distribute based on your Occupation and personal interests.

Skill Point Pools

  • Occupation Points: EDU × 2 + (Additional points from Occupation) — spend on Occupation skills only
  • Personal Interest Points: INT × 2 — spend on any skills you choose

Core Skills (partial list):

Accounting, Anthropology, Appraise, Archaeology, Art/Craft, Charm, Climb, Credit Rating, Cthulhu Mythos, Disguise, Dodge, Drive Auto, Fast Talk, Fighting (various), Firearms (various), First Aid, History, Intimidate, Jump, Language (various), Law, Library Use, Listen, Locksmith, Mechanical Repair, Medicine, Natural World, Navigate, Occult, Persuade, Psychoanalysis, Psychology, Ride, Science (various), Sleight of Hand, Spot Hidden, Stealth, Survival, Swim, Throw, Track
Important The skill Cthulhu Mythos begins at 0 and can only be increased through exposure to forbidden knowledge. Every point gained in Cthulhu Mythos permanently reduces your maximum Sanity by the same amount. Knowledge is a poison with no antidote.

Understanding Skill Checks

Call of Cthulhu uses a percentile system. Roll 1d100 (two ten-sided dice, one for tens, one for ones). To succeed, roll equal to or under your skill value.

⚀
Regular Success
Roll equal to or under your full skill value. This is a standard success.
⚁
Hard Success
Roll equal to or under half your skill value. A difficult task performed well.
⚂
Extreme Success
Roll equal to or under one-fifth your skill value. Exceptional performance under pressure.
⚃
Critical Success
Roll 01. Perfect execution — the best possible outcome.
⚅
Fumble
Roll 100, or 96–99 if skill is 50% or higher. Catastrophic failure with consequences.
✦ ✦ ✦

The Fragility of Sanity

Your Sanity Points equal your POW characteristic at the start. Witnessing the unnatural, reading forbidden texts, or encountering cosmic entities causes Sanity loss. This is measured in two ways:

  • Temporary Insanity: Lose 5+ Sanity in one encounter — roll on the Temporary Insanity table for immediate psychological breakdown
  • Indefinite Insanity: Lose 20% or more of current Sanity in one day — severe mental trauma requiring rest and treatment
  • Permanent Insanity: When current Sanity drops to 0, you are permanently insane and become an NPC
Sanity represents the mind's coherence in the face of cosmic horror
Each pip represents 5 Sanity Points. As they darken and fade, so does your grip on reality.

Sanity can be restored through rest, psychotherapy, or spending time in safe, comforting environments. However, maximum Sanity is reduced permanently by Cthulhu Mythos knowledge — there are truths from which the mind cannot recover.

Give your Investigator depth and history by answering these questions. Your answers inform roleplaying and provide the Keeper with narrative hooks.

1.

2.

3.

4.

7.

8.

Equipment & Resources

Your Credit Rating skill (0–99) determines your standard of living and starting cash. Most Investigators begin with modest means — enough for daily life, basic tools of their trade, and little more.

Typical Starting Equipment

  • Appropriate clothing for your Occupation and social standing
  • Tools of your profession (doctor's bag, camera, lockpicks, etc.)
  • A weapon if your background warrants it (typically a small revolver or knife)
  • Personal effects (pocket watch, wallet, keys, journal)
  • Transportation appropriate to your era and means (bicycle, automobile, etc.)
Historical Note The classic Call of Cthulhu setting is the 1920s, the Jazz Age — though campaigns may be set in any era. Adjust equipment, technology, and social attitudes accordingly. A Victorian gentleman carries a pocket watch; a modern investigator carries a smartphone.
✦ ✦ ✦

Establish Connections

No Investigator exists in isolation. Work with the Keeper and other players to establish relationships that tie you to the world and to each other.

  • Fellow Investigators: How do you know the other player characters? Colleagues? Friends? Chance acquaintances?
  • Organizations: Are you affiliated with Miskatonic University? A museum? A newspaper? The police?
  • Contacts: Who can you call for information, resources, or assistance? Librarians, former patients, underworld connections?
  • Dependents: Who relies on you? Spouse, children, aging parents, students?

These connections provide both motivation and vulnerability. The Mythos does not limit its depredations to you alone.

Make your Edgerunner — and try not to get flatlined.

Assign SkillsSTEP 04

Skills are purchased separately from stats. Each skill is tied to a governing stat and adds to it on a check. You roll 1d10 + Stat + Skill against a Difficulty Value (DV). Standard DV is 15 for most street-level tasks.

Distribute 86 skill points total. Skills cost 1 point per level up to 6, then 2 points per level from 7–10. Your Role grants you one skill at level 4 for free.

Run Your LifepathSTEP 06

The Lifepath system defines who your Edgerunner is before the campaign starts. Roll on the tables (or choose with your GM) to build a personal history full of the kind of complications that make Night City stories worth telling.

01
Cultural Origin
Where you come from and the cultural background that shaped your worldview. Affects starting language and cultural knowledge.
02
Personality
Your core traits: feelings toward people, valued possession, and the way you dress. These define your personal style on the Street.
03
Motivations
What you value most, what you feel about most people, and what you want out of life in a city that doesn't care about your wants.
04
Life Events
Roll five times on the Life Events table. These define major experiences — friends made, enemies earned, tragedies survived, fortunes lost.
05
Role-Specific Lifepath
Each Role has its own additional Lifepath tables — a Solo rolls on Background and Motive for Killing, a Rockerboy on Musical Style and Instruments.
06
Friends & Enemies
Your Lifepath generates specific NPCs — people who owe you, people who want you dead, and the complicated cases in between.

Gear & Starting EddiesSTEP 07

New Edgerunners start with 2,550 Eurodollars (eddies) plus any starting gear granted by their Role. Spend it on weapons, armor, cyberware, and survival equipment before hitting the streets.

Essential Starting Gear

  • Weapon: At minimum a handgun or melee weapon — anything else is an invitation to get flatlined immediately
  • Armor: Body armor (light, medium, or heavy) — your SP (Stopping Power) is subtracted from every hit that lands
  • Clothing: Night City is a fashion war zone. Your Wardrobe & Style skill isn't just vanity — it's reputation armor
  • Agent (smart phone): Your connection to the world — maps, contacts, limited NET access, and emergency calls
  • Medscanner or First Aid kit: Because medics cost eddies and the Street doesn't offer health insurance
  • Role-specific gear: Netrunners need a Cyberdeck; Techs need tools; Medtechs need their kit. Non-negotiable.
⚡ FIXER TIP Don't blow all your starting eddies on chrome. Cyberware is expensive and the Ripperdoc needs cash up front. Save enough to last your first few gigs — you can upgrade after you stop bleeding.

How the System WorksSTEP 08

Cyberpunk RED uses the Interlock System. When you try something with meaningful risk, roll 1d10 + Stat + Skill and meet or beat a Difficulty Value (DV).

DifficultyDVExample
Everyday9Fixing a common appliance, recalling basic info
Difficult15Hacking a corp terminal, treating a gunshot wound
Very Difficult21Bypassing military ICE, field surgery without equipment
Heroic24Surviving an ambush alone, decrypting black ICE
Legendary29Things even the best in Night City rarely pull off
Almost Impossible35The stuff of rumor and legend — if it even happened

Luck

Your LUCK stat is a pool of points that refreshes every session. Spend Luck points to add +1 to any roll, after you see the result. It's the difference between a cool story and a trip to the body bank.

Critical Injuries

Hits in combat don't just drain HP — certain damage thresholds force a Critical Injury roll. These produce specific wounds: cracked ribs, blinded eye, dismembered limb — each with mechanical penalties and narrative consequences. Night City leaves marks.

// CONTINUING //

Street Cred & RepSTEP 09

New Edgerunners start with Street Cred 0. Cred is the currency of reputation — it determines who takes your calls, who rents you gear, and who sends work your way.

  • Street Cred 1–4: Local. Nobody outside your neighborhood knows you exist.
  • Street Cred 5–9: Regional. Word is out. Fixers are calling. Corps are watching.
  • Street Cred 10–19: Night City wide. You're known. That's good and dangerous.
  • Street Cred 20+: Legend. You don't need to introduce yourself. That's also a target.

Cred is earned by completing gigs, making influential contacts, and doing things worth talking about. It also makes you a target — the higher you climb, the harder the corps and gangs work to knock you back down.

Finish Your FileSTEP 10

Personal Data

  • Handle (street name) — Never your real name. Never.
  • Real name — Known to fewer people the longer you survive
  • Age, appearance, and style — Night City is a visual culture. What does your chrome look like? What do you wear?
  • Current address — Coffin hotel? Street squat? Corporate dormitory? Nomad camp?

Your Crew

Work with the rest of the table to establish the group. Night City is brutal for solo operators. Define:

  • How did you all meet? (Shared gig? Same fixer? Survived something together?)
  • Who holds the group's eddies when someone's compromised?
  • Who is the fixer for your crew — or are you hiring out?
  • What does your crew call itself, if anything?

Your First Gig

The GM (called the Game Master or just GM in Cyberpunk RED) runs the city. Work with them to establish your crew's first gig. Gigs are the lifeblood of the game — corporate extraction, black-market smuggling, gang negotiation, or something worse. Night City always has work for people willing to get their hands dirty.

The corps own the city. The gangs own the streets. And you — you own nothing but your skills, your chrome, and your crew. Style over substance is a lie they tell you to keep you spending. In Night City, style IS substance. Look like prey and you become it. Now get out there and earn your rep before someone earns it for you.

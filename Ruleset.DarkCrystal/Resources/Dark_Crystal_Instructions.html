Character Creation

The Dark Crystal Adventure Game

Journey to Thra and become a Gelfling hero in the age before the Great Conjunction

Step 1Choose Your Clan

Every Gelfling belongs to one of the seven great clans, each with their own culture, territory, and traditions. Your clan shapes your abilities and worldview.

Vapra — The noble clan of Ha'rar, keepers of knowledge and the Crystal of Truth
Stonewood — Hardy warriors and guardians of the forests near the Castle
Grottan — Cave-dwelling mystics with exceptional dark-sight
Sifa — Seafaring traders and explorers of the Silver Sea
Dousan — Desert nomads who ride the Crystal Skimmers
Spriton — Farmers and cultivators of the Plains of Sog
Drenchen — Swamp-dwellers attuned to water and healing

Your clan grants you:

  • A Clan Ability usable once per session
  • Suggested traits that reflect your clan's culture
  • A connection to your clan's homeland on Thra

Step 2Define Your Traits

Traits are descriptive phrases that define your character's strengths, skills, background, and personality. They're used to gain advantages when performing relevant actions.

Creating Traits

You begin with 6 traits. Each trait should be a short, evocative phrase:

Good Trait Examples:
  • "Swift as a Fizzgig on the hunt"
  • "Raised in the Crystal Castle's libraries"
  • "Never backs down from a challenge"
  • "Knows every trading route across the Silver Sea"
  • "Can read the songs of the Cradle Tree"
Traits should be specific enough to be useful but broad enough to apply in multiple situations. "Good with animals" is better than "Can pet Fizzgigs" but not as interesting as "Speaks the ancient language of Arathim."

Types of Traits

Consider including traits from these categories:

  • Skills & Talents: What you're good at
  • Background & History: Where you came from and what you've done
  • Personality & Beliefs: Who you are and what drives you
  • Relationships: Important connections to others
  • Equipment & Resources: Special items or advantages you possess

Step 3Assign Motivation

Your motivation is your character's driving purpose — what compels them to adventure and face danger. It should be personal, meaningful, and something that can guide roleplay decisions.

Example Motivations:
  • Discover the truth about what the Skeksis are hiding
  • Reunite the scattered Gelfling clans
  • Protect my village from the darkening blight
  • Find my missing sibling taken by Garthim
  • Unlock the ancient wisdom of the First Gelfling
  • Prove that a Grottan can be as brave as any warrior clan

Your motivation can evolve during play as you discover new truths about Thra and the Crystal.

Step 4Determine Your Role

While not mechanically enforced, consider what role your character fills in a group:

  • Warrior: Protector and defender, skilled in combat
  • Scholar: Seeker of knowledge and ancient lore
  • Leader: Inspirer and coordinator of group efforts
  • Scout: Explorer and advance reconnaissance
  • Healer: Mender of wounds and keeper of life
  • Artisan: Crafter and builder of useful tools

Your traits should support your chosen role, though characters can be versatile and fill multiple functions.

Step 5Record Starting Resources

Hope

All characters begin with 3 Hope. Hope represents your inner light, determination, and connection to the Crystal of Truth. You can spend Hope to:

  • Add an extra die to any roll
  • Activate certain special abilities
  • Resist corruption or dark influence

Hope is restored through rest, inspiring moments, and acts of heroism that align with your motivation.

Health

You begin at full Health. The game uses a simple health track rather than numerical hit points. When you take damage, you mark conditions:

  • Hurt: Minor injuries (−1 die to physical actions)
  • Injured: Serious wounds (−2 dice to physical actions)
  • Dying: At death's door (unable to act without help)
The game focuses on narrative health rather than tracking individual hit points. Wounds are descriptive and have story consequences beyond just mechanical penalties.

Step 6Select Equipment & Possessions

Every Gelfling begins with basic adventuring gear appropriate to their clan and background. This typically includes:

  • Traveling clothes suited to your environment
  • A weapon if you're trained (dagger, staff, bow, or clan-specific weapon)
  • Basic supplies (rope, waterskin, provisions, light source)
  • Tools of your trade (musical instrument, artisan tools, healing herbs, etc.)
  • One or two special items that reflect your history or clan
Special Equipment: If you have an important item, consider making it a trait (like "Carries my mother's ancient lute" or "Wields a Stonewood guard's blade"). This makes it mechanically relevant in the story.

Step 7Establish Relationships

Determine your connections to other player characters and important NPCs in the world:

With Other Players

For each other PC, establish at least one connection:

  • How did you meet?
  • What do you admire or find challenging about them?
  • What shared experience bonds you?

Important NPCs

Work with your GM to identify:

  • Family members and where they are
  • Mentors or teachers who shaped you
  • Rivals or enemies
  • Allies in your home community
Relationships are vital in The Dark Crystal. The strength of community and bonds between Gelfling is a major theme. Your connections make you stronger and give you reasons to fight for Thra.

Step 8Physical Description & Details

Bring your character to life with descriptive details:

Appearance

  • Wings: All Gelfling have wings, though only females can truly fly. What do yours look like? (Color, pattern, size, any damage or unique features)
  • Hair: Style, color, decorations, or clan-specific adornments
  • Face: Distinctive features, expressions, marks, or tattoos
  • Build: Height, frame, distinctive physical characteristics
  • Clothing: How do you dress? What colors and styles do you prefer?

Personal Details

  • Age: Young, mature, or elder?
  • Gender: Male, female, or other
  • Name: Choose a name fitting your clan (Vapra names are elegant, Stonewood names are strong, Grottan names are soft, etc.)
  • Mannerisms: Quirks, habits, ways of speaking

Step 9Understand the Dreamfasting

All Gelfling possess the sacred ability to share memories through touch — this is called Dreamfasting.

When you touch another Gelfling (or certain other beings) and concentrate, you can:

  • Share visual memories and experiences
  • Communicate complex emotions and truths
  • Verify honesty (deception is nearly impossible during dreamfasting)
  • Pass along important knowledge or warnings
Dreamfasting is sacred and intimate. It's not used casually. When you dreamfast with another PC, describe what memory or feeling you share — it's a powerful roleplaying moment that deepens bonds between characters.

Step 10Final Preparation

Before beginning your adventure, complete these final steps:

Review with the GM

  • Ensure your traits are appropriate and balanced
  • Confirm your character fits the tone and era of the chronicle
  • Discuss how your motivation ties into the planned story
  • Establish your starting location on Thra

Establish Your "Normal"

Describe what your character's life was like before the adventure began:

  • Where did you live?
  • What was your daily routine?
  • What comfortable normalcy are you leaving behind?
  • What will you fight to protect or return to?

Session Zero Bonds

If you're creating characters together in a Session Zero, take time to:

  • Establish how the party knows each other
  • Create at least one shared memory or experience
  • Discuss party dynamics and potential sources of tension
  • Align on the group's overall goal or purpose

Remember: The Dark Crystal Adventure Game emphasizes narrative over complex mechanics. Your character is defined more by their story, relationships, and choices than by numbers and statistics. Embrace the wonder and mystery of Thra!

Generate Statistics

Roll your Statistics using percentile dice (d100). These represent your core abilities. Higher is better.

Statistic Roll Description
Strength (STR) 3D6 × 5 Physical power, melee damage
Constitution (CON) 3D6 × 5 Health, stamina, disease resistance
Dexterity (DEX) 3D6 × 5 Agility, reflexes, manual dexterity
Intelligence (INT) 2D6+6 × 5 Reasoning, memory, learning
Power (POW) 3D6 × 5 Willpower, mental strength, luck
Charisma (CHA) 3D6 × 5 Personality, leadership, persuasion
Hit Points: (STR + CON) ÷ 10 (round up)
Willpower: Equal to POW (starting)
Sanity: Equal to POW (starting) — THIS WILL DECREASE
Breaking Point: SAN - POW (when SAN drops below BP, trauma occurs)

Distribute Skill Points

Your Profession grants starting skill percentages. You also get personal interest points to spend: INT × 2. Distribute among any skills.

Core Skills

  • Alertness: Notice danger, spot clues, avoid ambush
  • Athletics: Running, jumping, climbing, swimming
  • Criminology: Crime scene investigation, forensics
  • Firearms: Handguns, rifles, shotguns
  • Foreign Language: Speak/read another language (specify)
  • HUMINT: Human intelligence gathering, reading people
  • Medicine: Treat wounds, diagnose illness, surgery
  • Melee Weapons: Knives, batons, improvised weapons
  • Occult: Knowledge of myths, rituals, forbidden lore
  • Persuade: Convince, intimidate, negotiate
  • Science: Biology, chemistry, physics (specify)
  • Search: Find hidden objects, research information
  • Stealth: Move quietly, hide, avoid detection
  • Unarmed Combat: Hand-to-hand fighting
⚠ OPERATIONAL NOTE Skills above 80% are suspicious. Delta Green recruits are competent but not superhuman. Exceptional skills draw attention. Stay under the radar.

Establish Bonds

Bonds are relationships that keep you sane. Family, friends, lovers. Each Bond starts with a score (usually CHA ÷ 2). When you lose Sanity, you can destroy a Bond instead to preserve your mind.

Example Bonds

  • Spouse/Partner: The person you come home to
  • Child: Your son or daughter — you want to protect them from the truth
  • Parent: Your mother or father — you owe them everything
  • Best Friend: Someone who knows you (but not Delta Green)
  • Mentor: Someone who trained you, guided you
  • Fellow Agent: Another Delta Green operative — they understand
When Bonds Break:
Every time you lose SAN, you can choose to reduce a Bond's score instead. When a Bond reaches 0, that relationship is destroyed. Your spouse divorces you. Your kids won't speak to you. Your friend calls the police.

This is the cost of serving Delta Green.

Why do you do this? What keeps you in the Program when you could walk away?

Common Motivations

  • Duty: Someone has to protect the world. Might as well be you.
  • Revenge: The unnatural took someone you loved. You'll make it pay.
  • Curiosity: You need to know the truth, no matter the cost.
  • Redemption: You failed before. This is your second chance.

  • Survival: You know too much. They'd kill you if you left.

Understand Sanity

SANITY SYSTEM
Your Sanity (SAN) represents your mental stability. It starts equal to your POW.
Every time you encounter the unnatural, you lose SAN. See a corpse animated by alien technology? Lose SAN. Watch your partner torn apart by a creature that shouldn't exist? Lose more SAN.

When SAN drops below your Breaking Point, you suffer trauma.
Temporary insanity. Disorders. Violent outbursts. Your character changes.

Sanity Loss Examples

  • See a dead body: 0/1D4 SAN
  • See a mutilated corpse: 1/1D6 SAN
  • See a friend die: 1D6/1D10 SAN
  • Encounter a Deep One: 1/1D6 SAN
  • Witness alien geometry: 1D6/1D20 SAN
  • Read the Necronomicon: 1D6/1D10 SAN (and gain Occult skill)
  • Summon an entity from beyond: 1D10/1D100 SAN
⚠ CRITICAL If SAN reaches 0, your character is permanently insane. They're institutionalized or dead. Roll a new character. This is not a game where everyone survives.

Mental Disorders

When SAN drops below your Breaking Point, you develop a Disorder. Roll or choose:

  • Addiction: Alcohol, drugs, anything to numb the pain
  • Depression: Nothing matters. You're just going through motions.
  • Paranoia: They're watching. They're everywhere. Trust no one.
  • Violence: You solve problems with force. Too much force.
  • Hypervigilance: You can't relax. Ever. Exhaustion sets in.
  • Flashbacks: You see it again. The thing. The blood. The screaming.
  • Dissociation: You feel disconnected from reality, yourself, others.

Disorders affect gameplay. They trigger in stress. They damage Bonds. They make you worse at your job.

Standard Loadout

Delta Green doesn't issue equipment. You use what your cover provides. FBI agents have bureau weapons. Military operators have military gear. Scientists have lab equipment.

Common Equipment

  • Body Armor: Kevlar vest (if appropriate to cover)

  • Communication: Cell phone, radio (encrypted)

Unnatural Weapons (Rarely Available)

  • Chemical Agents: Things that kill the unkillable
⚠ CLASSIFIED Use of unnatural weapons is unauthorized. Command will deny knowledge. You will be disavowed. Plausible deniability is mandatory.

Operational Procedures (Dice Rolls)

When attempting a task, roll d100 (percentile dice). Roll equal to or under your skill percentage to succeed.

> TASK RESOLUTION PROTOCOL STANDARD ROLL: • Roll d100 • Equal or under skill % = SUCCESS • Over skill % = FAILURE CRITICAL SUCCESS: Roll 01 — exceptional outcome CRITICAL FAILURE: Roll 00 or 100 — catastrophic failure OPPOSED ROLLS: Both parties roll. Higher success wins. Both fail? Nobody succeeds. DIFFICULTY MODIFIERS: • Easy: +20% to skill • Hard: -20% to skill • Impossible: -40% to skill (or just say no) > PROTOCOL COMPLETE

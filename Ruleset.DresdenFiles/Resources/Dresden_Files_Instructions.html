Create Your City

Before you make characters, create the city together. Dresden Files is about place. Where do you live? What supernatural forces control it? Who are the major players?

City Aspects (Choose 2-3)

  • Theme: What defines your city? ("Vegas Never Sleeps" / "Rust Belt Blues")
  • Threat: What danger looms? ("Red Court Controls Downtown" / "Wild Hunt Rides Monthly")
  • Location: Notable places ("Old Cathedral Hides Secrets" / "The Docks Are Neutral Ground")
City creation is collaborative! Everyone contributes. This makes the city yours. You'll care more about defending it when you've built it together.

Faces & Places

Define Faces (important NPCs) and Places (significant locations) for each major supernatural faction:

  • White Council: Who represents wizard authority?
  • Vampires: Red Court? White Court? Which court holds power?
  • Faerie: Summer or Winter more active? Who's the local representative?
  • Police/Mortal: Who knows about the supernatural?
  • Wild Cards: Werewolves? Demons? Outsiders?

Define High Concept

Your High Concept Aspect is a single phrase that sums up who you are. It's the elevator pitch for your character.

EXAMPLE
Wizard P.I. for Hire
You're a wizard who investigates supernatural crimes. Magic meets detective work. You consult for Chicago PD's Special Investigations.
"I'm like Harry Dresden, but cheaper and less dead."
EXAMPLE
Werewolf Enforcer
You're muscle for the local pack. When diplomacy fails, you step in. You're loyal, protective, and dangerous when crossed.
"The pack is family. Hurt them, answer to me."
EXAMPLE
Knight of the Cross
You wield one of three holy swords. God (or something) chose you to fight evil. You don't always understand why.
"Faith, hope, and a magical sword. Let's do this."
EXAMPLE
Cop Who Knows Too Much
You're mortal law enforcement who's seen the truth. Vampires are real. Magic exists. Your superiors think you're crazy.
"Protect and serve... even from things that shouldn't exist."

Choose Your Trouble

Your Trouble Aspect is your character's major problem. This drives conflict and gets you into (and out of) messes.

Example Troubles

  • "The White Council Doesn't Trust Me" — You broke the Laws of Magic once. They're watching.
  • "Hunger for More Power" — Magic is addictive. You want stronger spells. Dangerous path.
  • "Winter Court Debt" — You owe Mab a favor. She will collect. Eventually.
  • "My Girlfriend Is a Vampire" — Love is complicated. Love across species lines? Worse.
  • "Haunted By My Past" — You killed someone (or something). It left scars. Mental and physical.
  • "Too Curious For My Own Good" — You can't leave mysteries unsolved. Even when you should.

The Phase Trio

Create three more Aspects through collaborative storytelling. This is where FATE shines — your backstories intertwine.

Your First Adventure

Describe your first significant supernatural encounter. What happened? How did it change you? Write this as a one-sentence story title.

Example: "The Night the Vampires Came to Town"
Example: "Summoning Gone Wrong at the University"
Guest Starring in Someone Else's Story

Pick another player's Phase One story. How did you show up and help (or complicate things)? What Aspect do you gain from this crossover?

Example: "I Always Repay My Debts" (you helped them; now they owe you)
Another Guest Star Appearance

Pick a different player's Phase One story. How were you involved? This creates a web of connections. Your group is already linked.

Example: "Never Leave a Man Behind" (you saved someone; you're loyal)
The Phase Trio makes your group a group. You have shared history. You're not random strangers — you've already adventured together. This is brilliant design.

Key Skills

  • Conviction: Mental defense, resisting temptation, faith
  • Discipline: Mental control, casting spells under pressure
  • Lore: Knowledge of the supernatural, occult, magic theory
  • Contacts: Who you know, information gathering
  • Investigation: Finding clues, research, detective work
  • Alertness: Noticing danger, avoiding ambush
  • Fists/Weapons: Combat (punching or armed)
  • Deceit/Empathy: Lying or reading people

Choose Powers & Refresh

If you're supernatural, you have Powers. They cost Refresh (your starting Fate Points). Balance power with flexibility.

Supernatural Templates

  • Pure Mortal: No powers, but +2 Refresh (10 total). You're scrappy and resourceful.
  • Wizard: Channeling, Ritual, Thaumaturgy, The Sight. Powerful but limited by Laws of Magic.
  • Werewolf: Beast Change, Claws, Inhuman Strength/Speed. Full moon optional.
  • Vampire (White Court): Emotional Feeding, Superhuman abilities, Inhuman Recovery.
  • Fae-Touched: Glamours, Sponsored Magic (Summer/Winter), obligations to Courts.
  • Knight of the Cross: True Faith, Holy Touch, Item of Power (sword). Righteousness required.
  • Changeling: Half-fae. Some powers, but Choice looms (become full fae or mortal?).
REFRESH & FATE POINTS
You start with Refresh 8. Powers cost Refresh. Whatever's left becomes your starting Fate Points each session.

Example: Wizard with -6 in powers = 2 Refresh = Start each session with 2 Fate Points.
Example: Pure Mortal with no powers = 10 Refresh = Start each session with 10 Fate Points!

Fate Points let you invoke Aspects for bonuses. More powers = less points. Choose wisely.

Add Stunts

Stunts are special tricks or bonuses. You get stunts based on Refresh. Each character typically gets 1-3 stunts at creation.

Example Stunts

  • Ritual Specialist: +2 to Lore when preparing rituals with time
  • Killer Instinct: +2 to Weapons when ambushing surprised targets
  • Lies Upon Lies: +2 to Deceit when you've already established a cover story
  • Steady Hands: +2 to Discipline when controlling or targeting spells
  • Ear to the Ground: +2 to Contacts when gathering street-level information

Calculate Stress & Consequences

You have Physical and Mental stress tracks. When attacked, mark boxes. When boxes fill up, you take Consequences (injuries/trauma).

Physical Stress: Based on Endurance skill
• Average (0-1): 2 boxes
• Fair (2): 3 boxes
• Good (3-4): 4 boxes

Mental Stress: Based on Conviction skill
• Average (0-1): 2 boxes
• Fair (2): 3 boxes
• Good (3-4): 4 boxes

Consequences: Everyone gets 3 consequence slots:
• Mild (2-shift): "Bruised Ribs"
• Moderate (4-shift): "Concussion"
• Severe (6-shift): "Shattered Leg"

Using Aspects

Aspects are the heart of FATE. They're always true, and you can invoke them for bonuses by spending Fate Points.

Invoking Your Aspects

  • Spend 1 Fate Point: +2 to a roll OR re-roll the dice
  • Your Aspects: "Wizard P.I. for Hire" applies to investigation using magic
  • Scene Aspects: "Burning Building" — invoke for +2 to intimidate/escape
  • Other's Aspects: Invoke enemy's "Overconfident" for +2 to trick them

Compels (Getting Fate Points)

The GM (or other players) can Compel your Aspects to make your life harder. Accept the complication, get a Fate Point. Refuse, pay a Fate Point.

Example Compel: Your Trouble is "Winter Court Debt." The GM says: "Mab's messenger appears, demanding payment NOW — right in the middle of your investigation. This complicates things. Accept?"

Say yes → get Fate Point. Say no → pay Fate Point.

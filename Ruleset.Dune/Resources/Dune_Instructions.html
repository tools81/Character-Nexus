Define Your Drives

Drives are core motivations that define your character. Choose three: Duty, Faith, Justice, Power, Truth, or Wealth. When you act according to a Drive, you can regain Determination.

  • Duty: Loyalty to House, Emperor, or cause above self-interest
  • Faith: Belief in religion, prophecy, or spiritual truth
  • Justice: Righting wrongs, punishing the guilty, protecting the innocent
  • Power: Accumulating influence, control, and authority
  • Truth: Seeking knowledge, exposing lies, understanding reality
  • Wealth: Accumulating resources, securing financial power

Determination & Momentum

DETERMINATION
Determination represents your willpower and resolve. Start with 1 Determination. Spend it to re-roll dice, perform heroic actions, or resist threats. Regain by acting according to your Drives or accepting complications.

Momentum

Momentum is the shared resource pool for your group. When you roll well, generate Momentum. Spend it to:

  • Gain additional dice on tests
  • Obtain information
  • Create advantages
  • Activate special abilities

But beware — the GM has Threat, their counter-resource. Complications add to Threat. Balance success with caution.

The 2d20 System

When you attempt something risky, roll 2d20. You're trying to roll equal to or under your Attribute + Skill. Each success counts.

⟡ MENTAT ANALYSIS ⟡ Target Number (TN): Your Attribute + Skill (e.g., Communicate 9 + Diplomacy 2 = TN 11).
Roll 2d20: Each die showing ≤ TN = 1 success. Most tasks need 1 success.
Difficulty: GM sets difficulty (0-5 successes needed).
Complications: Roll 20 = complication. Something goes wrong even on success.
Momentum: Extra successes beyond what's needed generate Momentum for the group.

Assets & Traits

Characters may have Assets — advantages that grant bonus dice. These include equipment, contacts, resources, or circumstances.

Creating Assets

  • Spend Momentum: Create an Asset by spending 1-2 Momentum
  • Asset Grants: +1d20 to relevant tests
  • Examples: "High Ground," "Trusted Informant," "Ornithopter," "Poison Snooper"
  • Duration: Assets last for a scene unless specified otherwise

Traits

Traits are descriptors — character aspects, item qualities, or situation features. Use Traits creatively to create Assets or add narrative flavor.

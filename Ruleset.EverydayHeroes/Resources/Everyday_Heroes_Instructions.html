Everyday Heroes

CHARACTER CREATION — CORE RULEBOOK v1.0  |  d20 MODERN SYSTEM

Everyday Heroes is a cinematic action RPG built on the d20 system and powered by the 5E OGL. You don't play a wizard or an elf — you play a soldier, a spy, a wheelman, or an ordinary person thrust into extraordinary circumstances. Think action movies. Think grit.

Choose Your Concept

Before touching your character sheet, crystallize a clear mental picture of who your hero is. Everyday Heroes characters are grounded in cinematic realism — ordinary (and extraordinary) people shaped by profession, circumstance, and will.

Ask yourself:

  • Genre: What kind of action movie are you in? Spy thriller? Grindhouse? Military shooter? Disaster survival?
  • Hook: What is the one defining thing about your character that makes them memorable?
  • Motivation: What do they want badly enough to risk their life for?
  • Cost: What have they already sacrificed to get here?
// Director's Note Your GM is called the Game Master (GM) or sometimes the Director. Work with them to ensure your concept fits the campaign's tone and setting — whether that's a gritty 80s action pastiche, a modern espionage thriller, or a post-apocalyptic survival story.

Select Your Background

Your Background represents your life before becoming a hero — your civilian career, upbringing, and the skills you bring from that past. Backgrounds provide skill proficiencies, tool proficiencies, starting equipment, and a unique Background Feature.

Example Backgrounds
  • Criminal / Contact: You have a history of breaking the law and a network of shady contacts
  • Entertainer: You've performed for audiences — and know how to command a room
  • Folk Hero: Ordinary circumstances made you an icon in your community
  • Military Veteran: Service has made you disciplined, resourceful, and battle-hardened
  • Noble / Elite: Wealth and privilege opened doors — and made enemies
  • Outlander: You come from beyond civilization, with survival instincts honed by necessity
  • Sage / Academic: Institutional knowledge is your power — archives and expertise
  • Urchin: The streets raised you fast — stealth, street-smarts, and contacts

Each Background also provides your character with Personality Traits, an Ideal, a Bond, and a Flaw — use these or create your own to flesh out who you are.

Choose Your Class

Your Class is the defining role you fill — your genre archetype, your action-movie identity. Every Class provides hit dice, proficiencies, starting equipment, and a suite of cinematic abilities. You pick a subclass (Archetype) within your Class at 3rd level.

Soldier
Trained for combat and tactical operations. Weapons are your language; the battlefield is your classroom.
Martial Artist
Your body is the weapon. Years of discipline have forged you into a finely-tuned fighting machine.
Spy
Information, infiltration, and misdirection. You work in the shadows and thrive on secrets.
Wheelman
Behind the wheel — or the controls of anything that moves — you are supernaturally skilled.
Gadgeteer
Technology is your edge. You build, modify, and improvise devices that give your team a decisive advantage.
Medic
You keep people alive under fire — medic, combat surgeon, or field doctor with ice in your veins.
Sniper
Patience, distance, precision. One shot, one kill — and the ability to vanish before they find you.
Tough
You absorb punishment that would kill anyone else and keep coming. Resilience made flesh.
// Archetype Note Each Class has multiple Archetypes (subclasses) that specialize your role — for example, a Soldier might choose Commando, Green Beret, or Ranger. Archetypes are selected at 3rd level, so at character creation you simply choose your base Class.

Generate Ability Scores

Everyday Heroes uses the same six ability scores as classic d20 systems. These are the foundation of every roll you make.

STR
Strength
Brute force, melee attacks, and carrying capacity
DEX
Dexterity
Reflexes, finesse, ranged attacks, and stealth
CON
Constitution
Endurance, hit points, and physical toughness
INT
Intelligence
Knowledge, technical skills, and reasoning
WIS
Wisdom
Perception, intuition, and street sense
CHA
Charisma
Persuasion, intimidation, leadership, and presence

Score Generation Methods

Method How It Works
Standard Array Assign 15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8 to your six scores in any order
Point Buy Start all scores at 8. Spend 27 points to raise them (costs increase at higher values)
Dice Rolling Roll 4d6 and drop the lowest die, six times. Assign freely

After assigning scores, apply any racial or background modifiers, then calculate your Ability Modifiers ((Score − 10) ÷ 2, rounded down).

Skills & Proficiencies

Your Class and Background each grant you proficiency in a number of skills. Add your Proficiency Bonus (+2 at 1st level) to any check using a skill you're proficient in.

Weapon & Tool Proficiencies

Your Class determines which modern weapons you're trained with — from handguns and rifles to heavy ordnance and explosives. Backgrounds may add tool proficiencies (lockpicks, mechanic's tools, hacking hardware, etc.).

Choose Cinematic Talents

Cinematic Talents are Everyday Heroes' version of feats — special abilities that represent your hero's signature moves, training, and action-movie style. You select one at 1st level (or in place of an Ability Score Improvement as you advance).

Sample Cinematic Talents
  • Action Hero: Once per short rest, you can reroll a failed saving throw and keep the new result
  • Combat Medic: You can use a bonus action to stabilize a dying ally and grant them 1 HP
  • Dual Wielder: You can engage with two weapons simultaneously with only a minor off-hand penalty
  • Evasive: When you're not incapacitated, attackers have disadvantage on opportunity attacks against you
  • Gun Fu: When you take the Attack action with a firearm, you may make a melee attack as a bonus action
  • Hard to Kill: When you drop to 0 HP, roll a CON save (DC 10 + damage taken above 0). On success, stay at 1 HP
  • MacGyver: You can craft improvised tools and weapons from available materials in the field
  • One-Liner: Once per combat, deal extra psychic damage with a devastating comeback
  • Spray and Pray: Make a suppressive-fire attack that affects a cone area
  • Tactical Driver: You can make vehicle attacks as part of the same action as a regular attack

Calculate Hit Points & Defense

Hit Points

At 1st level, your HP equals your Class's Hit Die maximum plus your Constitution modifier.

d6Gadgeteer, Spy
d8Martial Artist, Medic, Wheelman
d10Sniper, Soldier
d12Tough

Armor Class (Defense)

  • Unarmored: 10 + Dexterity modifier
  • Light Armor: Armor value + Dexterity modifier (max +2)
  • Medium Armor: Armor value + Dexterity modifier (max +5)
  • Heavy Armor: Fixed armor value (no Dexterity modifier)

Trauma

Everyday Heroes introduces a Trauma system alongside hit points. Massive damage, critical failures, or psychologically devastating events can impose Trauma Conditions — narrative impairments representing the real cost of a life spent in the action.

Starting Equipment

Choose starting gear from your Class's options or roll for it. Equipment in Everyday Heroes is purpose-built for the modern world.

Weapon Categories

  • Melee: Combat knives, batons, improvised weapons, martial arts weapons
  • Handguns: Pistols, revolvers — concealable, versatile
  • Rifles: Assault rifles, battle rifles, precision rifles for range and stopping power
  • Shotguns: Close-range devastation
  • SMGs: Compact, high rate of fire, close-quarters
  • Explosives: Grenades, breaching charges, C4 — controlled demolition
  • Heavy Weapons: LMGs, RPGs, crew-served weapons for the biggest threats

Gear & Equipment

  • Body armor appropriate to your Class proficiencies
  • Communication devices (earpiece, radio, encrypted phone)
  • Vehicles relevant to your Class (Wheelman starts with one)
  • Class-specific tools (medical kit, hacking rig, explosives kit, etc.)
  • Background equipment from your pre-hero life
// Signature Gear Consider designating one item as your Signature Piece — the weapon or tool most associated with your character's identity. This is a narrative choice that the GM may use to create dramatic moments if you're ever separated from it.

Define Your Personality

Everyday Heroes characters have mechanical personality anchors drawn from (or inspired by) your Background. These aren't just flavour — they can earn you Inspiration when you play to them.

The Four Anchors
  • Personality Trait: A specific habit, mannerism, or attitude that colors how you act ("I never leave a man behind." / "I always have an exit strategy.")
  • Ideal: A core principle you believe in, regardless of cost ("Justice must be served, no matter who you are." / "The mission always comes first.")
  • Bond: A person, organization, or cause that ties you to the world and gives you something to lose ("I'll do anything to protect my sister." / "My unit is my family.")
  • Flaw: A weakness, vice, or blind spot that creates drama and trouble ("I can't walk away from a fight even when I should." / "I trust no one — not even my team.")

Final Details & Connections

Personal Details

  • Name & Alias: Real name and any cover identities
  • Age, appearance, and distinguishing marks
  • Background narrative: The 3-sentence origin that explains how you got here
  • Cover Story: What does the average civilian think you do for work?

Team Connections

Work with the group to establish at least one connection to each other PC:

  • How did you first meet? Was it a job, a crisis, or something personal?
  • What do you owe them — or what do they owe you?
  • Is there an unresolved tension, secret, or shared trauma?

Campaign Hooks

Give the GM at least two usable hooks from your backstory — enemies who might resurface, people you're trying to protect, debts still owed. The best action heroes always have unfinished business.

Ready to Roll Everyday Heroes strips away the fantasy and magic to ask a simpler question: when it all goes wrong, what kind of person are you? Your character sheet is your answer. Now go find out if it's enough.

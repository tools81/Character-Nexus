War. War never changes. But you do. This guide will take you from vault-dweller to wastelander — creating your survivor using the 2D20 system adapted for the post-nuclear world of Fallout. Everything runs on the legendary S.P.E.C.I.A.L. system, as the Overseer intended.

1Choose Your Character Concept

Before touching your character sheet, decide who your survivor is. The Wasteland is vast and merciless — your concept will anchor every decision that follows.

  • Where did they come from? Vault? Wasteland-born? Tribal? Enclave remnant? Institute refugee?
  • What do they want? Revenge, safety, power, answers, family — or just another meal?
  • What broke them? Every wastelander has scars. What are yours?
  • What's their code? Do they have one? How far will they bend it?
⚠ Overseer Advisory Discuss the campaign setting with your Game Master before finalizing your concept. The timeframe, region, and factions active in the wasteland will shape what backstories are plausible.

2Select Your Origin

Your Origin describes your life before the campaign begins — where you came from and the experiences that forged you. It grants starting skills, equipment, and a unique Origin Perk.

◈
Vault Dweller
Born inside one of Vault-Tec's shelters. Educated, naïve, and equipped with pre-war knowledge — but unprepared for the surface.
◈
Wastelander
Surface-born and survival-hardened. You know the Wasteland's rhythms and its cruelties. You've never known anything else.
◈
Brotherhood of Steel
Former Initiate or Knight of the Brotherhood. Disciplined, tech-focused, and dragging the baggage of an isolationist ideology.
◈
Ghoul
You survived the bombs — barely. Radiation-scarred but immortal, you carry centuries of memory and the weight of a world that's afraid of you.
◈
Super Mutant
The FEV changed you into something massive and feared. Dim memories of your former self and a body built for war.
◈
Synth (Gen 3)
An Institute creation indistinguishable from human — except to yourself. Questions of consciousness, identity, and freedom haunt every step.
◈
Robot / Mister Handy
A pre-war machine still operating on old directives — or one who has evolved a personality all their own over two centuries of operation.

3Assign S.P.E.C.I.A.L. Attributes

The S.P.E.C.I.A.L. system is the bedrock of every Fallout character. Distribute 40 points among the seven attributes. Each attribute has a base of 1 and a maximum of 10 at character creation (some Origins may modify this).

S
Strength
Melee damage, carry weight, and raw physical power
P
Perception
Awareness, ranged accuracy, and picking up on details
E
Endurance
HP, damage resistance, and tolerating radiation & poison
C
Charisma
Persuasion, companion morale, and social manipulation
I
Intelligence
Skill points, tech checks, and medical knowledge
A
Agility
Action Points, stealth, and initiative
L
Luck
Critical hits, Luck Points, and the universe's general attitude toward you

All checks use 2D20. Roll under your Attribute + Skill target number to score hits. Each die that rolls equal to or under the target generates 1 success. Rolling under your Attribute alone scores a critical success (a SPECIAL check). You need at least 1 success to pass. Additional successes can be spent on Momentum for extra effects.

4Calculate Derived Statistics

These secondary values are calculated from your S.P.E.C.I.A.L. attributes and are updated as your character changes.

Statistic Formula Effect

Melee Bonus STR ÷ 2 Added damage on unarmed/melee hits

5Assign Skills

Skills represent training and experience in specific areas. Each skill is tied to a governing attribute. At character creation, you receive skill points based on Intelligence and your Origin.

Skills start at 0 and can be raised to a maximum of 6. The target number for any check is Attribute + Skill (maximum 20).

Athletics Barter Big Guns Energy Weapons Explosives Lockpick Medicine Melee Weapons Pilot Repair Science Small Guns Sneak Speech Survival Throwing Unarmed

Choose 3 Tag Skills at character creation. Tagged skills receive a +2 bonus to all checks and are marked on your character sheet as your areas of focus. Your Origin may restrict or expand which skills can be tagged.

6Select Your Perks

Perks are the special abilities that truly define your wastelander's playstyle. You receive 1 Perk at character creation plus your Origin Perk. Additional Perks are gained as you level up.

Each Perk has a Perk Rank requirement — some cannot be taken until you have reached a certain level and have enough points in the relevant S.P.E.C.I.A.L. attribute.

Adamantium Skeleton
Radiation or the hard life of the Wasteland has made your bones practically indestructible. Reduce limb damage taken by 2.
Gun Nut
You can modify and maintain firearms at a higher level than most. Unlocks advanced weapon mod options and reduces repair difficulty.
Lone Wanderer
Traveling without companions suits you. Gain bonus damage and damage resistance when operating without NPCs in your party.
Rad Resistant
Exposure has built a tolerance. Increase your Radiation Threshold significantly and reduce ongoing radiation effects.
Scrounger
You have a knack for finding resources others miss. Increase the quality and quantity of loot found during scavenging.
Sneak
Move without sound through the Wasteland. Gain bonuses to Sneak checks and deal extra damage on attacks from stealth.
Strong Back
You can carry significantly more than your build suggests. Increase carry weight by 25 lbs and ignore the first overencumbrance penalty.

7Starting Equipment & Caps

Your starting gear comes from a combination of your Origin and any Perks that grant items. The Game Master may also provide a starting cap amount based on the campaign's tone.

Typical Starting Loadout

  • Primary Weapon: One weapon appropriate to your Origin and tagged skills
  • Armor: Light armor or clothing (Vault Suit for Vault Dwellers, etc.)
  • Consumables: A handful of Stimpaks, Rad-X, and RadAway
  • Survival Gear: Canteen, bedroll, basic tools
  • Caps: Typically 50–200 bottle caps as starting currency (GM discretion)

Condition & Maintenance

All weapons and armor have a Condition track. Equipment degrades with use and must be repaired with the Repair skill and appropriate materials. Broken gear suffers significant penalties or ceases to function entirely.

⚠ Weight Matters Track your carry weight carefully. Exceeding your limit imposes stacking penalties to Agility-based skills and movement. The Wasteland is unforgiving to those who can't move quickly.

8Understand Momentum & Luck

Momentum

When your 2D20 roll generates more successes than needed, the excess become Momentum. Momentum can be spent immediately or saved into the group's shared Momentum pool (maximum 6).

Spend Momentum to:

  • Perform additional actions in combat
  • Ask the Game Master for information
  • Reduce the time taken on a task
  • Create advantages for allies
  • Deal bonus damage (+1 damage die per point spent)

Luck Points

Your personal Luck Point pool (equal to LCK ÷ 2) refreshes each session. Spend Luck Points to:

  • Reroll any single die on a check
  • Activate your Luck-based Perks
  • Survive a lethal hit (once per session)

Complications

Rolling a 19 or 20 on any die generates a Complication — a narrative setback determined by the Game Master, regardless of whether the overall check succeeded. The Wasteland always has a way of biting back.

9Optional: Choose Traits

Traits are optional double-edged modifiers from the classic Fallout games. Taking a Trait gives you a meaningful benefit and a matching drawback. You may take up to 2 Traits at character creation.

  • Bloody Mess: You deal more damage but your kills are always grotesquely spectacular — drawing attention
  • Fast Shot: All your ranged attacks cost 1 fewer AP, but you cannot target specific body locations
  • Finesse: Your attacks are more likely to score critical hits, but deal reduced base damage
  • Heavy Handed: Crushing melee power, but you never score critical hits in melee
  • Kamikaze: You always act first in combat, but your Defense is permanently reduced
  • Small Frame: Your Agility is boosted, but your limbs are more easily crippled
  • Wild Wasteland: The world is stranger and more bizarre for you — expect unusual encounters

10Finishing Touches

Personal Details

  • Name: Full name and any wasteland alias
  • Apparent Age & Physical Description
  • Pre-War Occupation (if applicable — or tribe / vault number)
  • Notable Scars, Mutations, or Modifications

Connections & Bonds

  • Establish at least one person your character would risk their life to protect
  • Name one enemy or rival already lurking somewhere in the Wasteland
  • Define what faction (if any) you feel allegiance to — or fear

Goals

  • Immediate: What does your character want before the end of the first session?
  • Long-Term: What would make your character feel their story has meant something?

The Wasteland doesn't care about your plans. It doesn't care about your past. It only cares what you do when everything goes wrong — and everything will go wrong. Your Pip-Boy is charged. Your S.P.E.C.I.A.L. stats are locked. The vault door is opening. Good luck out there. You're going to need it.

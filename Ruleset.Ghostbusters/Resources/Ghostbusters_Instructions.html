1Operative Concept [ SEE INSTRUCTIONS REVERSE ]

Ghostbusters International is a light, comedic RPG built for fast, funny paranormal adventures. Before filling in any numbers, decide the kind of ghostbuster you are. The game leans into farce, slapstick, and the absurdity of billing ghosts by the hour.

  • Archetype: The brainiac? The musclehead? The coward who screams first? The businessperson who's here for the invoicing?
  • Comedic Hook: What's your character's signature source of trouble? Over-confidence? Total incompetence in one area? Pathological cheapness?
  • Why Are You Here? The pay is terrible, the work is dangerous, and your equipment smells like ectoplasm. So why?
NOTE FROM OPERATIONS Ghostbusters is not a game about brooding antiheroes. The tone is the original films: competent in the chaos, overwhelmed by the weird, and usually arguing about money. Embrace the comedy.

2Assign Your Four Traits

Every Ghostbuster is defined by four core Traits. Distribute 12 points among them however you like, with a minimum of 1 and a maximum of 5 in any single Trait at character creation.

Brains
Science, knowledge, investigation, piloting, and anything requiring actual intelligence
Muscles
Fighting, lifting, carrying, intimidating, and surviving things that should kill you
Moves
Speed, stealth, dexterity, dodging, and getting out of the way of proton streams
Cool
Persuasion, deception, willpower, keeping it together when face-to-face with a Class VII apparition

Multiply each Trait by 3 to get its Brownie Point value ‚Äî the number you'll be rolling against during play. You'll be rolling a six-sided die and trying to roll equal to or under your target number.

3Choose Your Talents

Talents are specific areas of expertise within your Traits. Each Talent is tied to one of your four Traits and gives you a +3 bonus to your Brownie Point target when the Talent applies. You start with 3 Talents.

Talents are player-invented descriptors, not a fixed list ‚Äî write something that fits your character. Below are examples to spark ideas:

B

Academic knowledge of ghosts, spectral entities, and the supernatural ‚Äî the kind you get from reading, not from field experience
B

You've actually read the manual. You know which settings cause a nuclear explosion and try to avoid them
M

Whether it's a ghost trap, a colleague, or a piece of furniture ‚Äî if it can be thrown, you're the one to throw it
M

You've developed an improbable resistance to being knocked down, sat upon, or otherwise physically inconvenienced by apparitions
V

You can navigate New York traffic, jump curbs, and park illegally outside a haunted building with smooth professionalism
V

C

Convincing terrified people to sign a contract, pay a deposit, and not sue you when you destroy their kitchen
C

Sometimes the job requires not alerting the ghost. You can, with effort, suppress the scream. Most of the time

4How the Dice Work

Ghostbusters uses a beautifully simple system. When you attempt something, your Game Master (called the Ghost Master) decides which Trait applies and sets a difficulty. You roll one or more six-sided dice and try to roll equal to or under your Trait √ó 3 (your Brownie Points).

Situation How It Works
Simple Task Roll 1d6, beat your Brownie Points ‚Äî no modifier
With Talent Add +3 to your Brownie Points for this roll
Hard Task Ghost Master subtracts from your Brownie Points (difficulty 3, 6, 9, etc.)
The Ghost Die One die in any roll is the special Ghost Die ‚Äî if it shows the ghost symbol (6), something goes wrong regardless of success
Brownie Points Also the reward currency ‚Äî earn them for clever, funny, or heroic play. Spend to improve Traits and Talents
GHOST MASTER'S REMINDER The Ghost Die is the heart of the system. A roll that scores a 6 on the Ghost Die means something complicates the situation ‚Äî even if you succeeded. This is how a perfectly good ghost containment unit ends up venting ectoplasm into the break room.

5Define Your Goal

Every Ghostbuster has a personal Goal ‚Äî a specific ambition they're working toward. This isn't just flavour: your Goal is the primary way you earn Brownie Points (the game's advancement currency) between sessions.

Goal Examples
  • Get rich enough to never wear this jumpsuit again
  • Prove that Ghostbusting is a legitimate scientific discipline
  • Capture a full Class VII before retiring
  • Earn enough to pay off my student loans by age forty
  • Make it through a single job without being covered in ectoplasm
  • Build a proton pack from scratch without anyone dying
  • Get the Mayor to officially recognize our franchise
  • Find the ghost that haunted my grandmother's kitchen and evict it once and for all

Goals should be specific and achievable within a session or two. Once you accomplish a Goal, choose a new one. The Ghost Master awards extra Brownie Points when you make real progress.

7Earning & Spending Brownie Points

Brownie Points serve dual purpose: they're your task resolution target numbers (Trait √ó 3) and the game's advancement and reward currency.

Earning Brownie Points

  • Completing Jobs: Successfully busting a ghost and billing the client earns points for the whole team
  • Advancing Your Goal: Making meaningful progress toward your personal Goal earns bonus points
  • Playing in Character: Good roleplaying, funny moments, and heroic decisions at personal cost earn points at the Ghost Master's discretion
  • Team Contribution: Helping other players succeed in memorable ways

Spending Brownie Points

  • Improve a Trait: Raise one Trait by 1 point (cost = new level √ó 10 Brownie Points)
  • Add a Talent: Purchase a new Talent for 10 Brownie Points
  • Improve a Talent: Raise a Talent's bonus from +3 to a higher bonus (rare)

8Sanity & Survival

Ghostbusters doesn't use traditional hit points. Instead, the consequences of failure are narrative and comedic ‚Äî you get covered in slime, embarrassed in front of a client, fired from a bridge, etc.

However, severe paranormal encounters can test your mental composure. The Cool Trait measures your resistance to supernatural terror. Failing Cool checks when confronted with truly horrific entities can result in:

  • Panic: Your character acts irrationally ‚Äî runs, hides, or does something spectacularly unhelpful
  • Temporary Brownie Point Loss: Your shaken confidence imposes a temporary penalty
  • Ghost Master Complications: Something amusing and inconvenient happens as a result of your failure of nerve
HEALTH & SAFETY NOTICE Physical damage in Ghostbusters is handled the same way. Getting blasted by a ghost, falling off a building, or having a chandelier dropped on you doesn't kill you ‚Äî it just results in funny, inconvenient outcomes that further complicate the job. This is that kind of game.

9Your Franchise

Ghostbusters International is a franchise operation. Your team is either working out of the original NYC HQ or running a licensed regional franchise. Work with your Ghost Master to establish:

  • Location: What city or region does your franchise cover? (Or are you at the original New York base?)
  • The Firehouse: Describe your HQ. Is it a converted firehouse? A strip mall between a dry cleaner and a nail salon? A condemned office building you got suspiciously cheap?
  • The Ecto Vehicle: Every franchise has a vehicle. Converted ambulance? Modified station wagon? What's yours called and what's wrong with it?
  • Containment Unit: Where do captured ghosts go? Is it well-maintained or quietly terrifying?
  • Finances: Are you solvent? Do you owe money to someone the team should probably be afraid of? What does your current monthly budget look like?

Select Code Name & Specialty

Every G.I. Joe operative is identified by a Code Name — a callsign that defines your reputation and role. Your Specialty is your military occupational classification (MOS) — what you're trained to do.

Code Name Guidelines

  • Should reflect your specialty, personality, or combat style
  • One or two words, memorable and distinctive
  • Examples: Snake Eyes, Scarlett, Duke, Roadblock, Shipwreck, Gung-Ho

Determine Rank

Your Military Rank reflects your experience and position in the command structure. Starting characters typically begin as E-5 (Sergeant) or equivalent, though special circumstances may warrant higher or lower rank.

Rank Grade Notes
Private / Airman E-1 to E-3 Basic training complete, learning specialty
Corporal / Specialist E-4 Experienced in specialty, may lead small teams
Sergeant E-5 Squad leader, tactical decision-maker — standard PC rank
Staff Sergeant E-6 Platoon-level NCO, coordinates multiple squads
Lieutenant O-1 to O-2 Junior officer, leads platoons or specialized units
Captain / Major O-3 to O-4 Company/battalion command, strategic planning

How Rolling Works

The G.I. Joe RPG uses a D6 dice pool system. When you attempt something challenging, roll your Attribute dice + any Skill dice. The Wild Die adds drama and risk.

1
Roll Your Pool
Add Attribute + Skill dice together. Roll them all. Add up the total.
2
The Wild Die
One die in your pool is a different color — the Wild Die. If it shows 6, roll it again and add. If it shows 1, remove the highest die from your total.
3
Beat the Difficulty
Your total must equal or exceed the Difficulty Number set by the GM. Standard difficulty is 10.
4
Success & Failure
Meet or beat the DN = success. Fall short = failure. Beating DN by 10+ = exceptional success.
⚠ WILD DIE COMPLICATION The Wild Die keeps every roll tense. Roll a 6, you're a hero. Roll a 1, something goes wrong — even if you succeed overall. This is by design. War is unpredictable.

Wounds & Combat

Damage is measured in Wound Levels. When you take a hit, the attacker's damage roll is compared to your Strength + armor. The difference determines severity.

Wound Level Effect
Stunned No damage but lose next action
Wounded -1D to all actions until treated
Severely Wounded -2D to all actions, may become incapacitated
Incapacitated Cannot act, will die without immediate medical attention
Mortally Wounded Death imminent — say your last words

Multiple wounds at the same level advance you to the next level. Combat in G.I. Joe is dangerous — body armor and cover are not optional.

Standard Issue Equipment

All G.I. Joe operatives receive standard field gear. Your Specialty determines additional equipment available for requisition.

Universal Kit

  • M16A2 Assault Rifle or equivalent primary weapon (Damage 5D, Range 50m)
  • M1911 Sidearm (Damage 4D, Range 10m)
  • Combat Knife (Damage STR+1D)
  • Body Armor (+2D vs. physical damage)
  • Field Uniform (woodland or desert camouflage)
  • Web Gear (ammunition pouches, canteen, first aid kit)
  • Radio Communicator (squad frequency)
  • Rations (3 days)

Specialty Equipment (Examples)

  • Sniper: M24 Sniper Rifle (Damage 6D, Range 300m), spotter scope, ghillie suit
  • Heavy Weapons: M60 Machine Gun (Damage 6D, sustained fire), extra ammo belts
  • Medic: Advanced medical kit (+2D to First Aid), stretcher, morphine
  • Engineer: C-4 explosives, detonators, wire cutters, toolkit
  • Pilot: Flight suit, survival kit, flare gun, ejection seat (hopefully never needed)

Know Your Enemy

Cobra is a ruthless terrorist organization determined to rule the world. Led by the mysterious Cobra Commander, they deploy advanced weaponry, mind-control technology, and fanatical troops against freedom.

Common Cobra Forces

  • Cobra Troopers: Faceless infantry — expendable, numerous, poorly trained but well-armed
  • Crimson Guard: Elite soldiers — disciplined, dangerous, personally loyal to Cobra Commander
  • Vipers: Special operations — skilled in various specialties (helicopter pilots, shock troops, saboteurs)
  • B.A.T.s: Battle Android Troopers — robots with built-in weapons, no fear, no mercy

Your mission is simple: stop them. The methods are up to you — and command approval.

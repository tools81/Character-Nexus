You saw something you weren't supposed to see. Now you can't unsee it.

You were normal. You had a life — job, family, bills, routines. Then one night you saw them. The things that hide in plain sight. Vampires. Werewolves. Worse things without names. And when you saw them, something changed. You were Imbued — chosen by forces you don't understand to fight monsters you can't explain. Welcome to the Hunt. You can't go back.

The Imbuing

You didn't choose this. Something chose you. In a moment of crisis — witnessing a murder, stumbling on a feeding, being in the wrong place at the wrong time — you received the Imbuing. Your eyes opened. You see the monsters now. All of them. And you feel the call to hunt.

Assign Virtues

Every Hunter has three Virtues rated from 1 to 5. These measure your moral strength and fuel your Edges. Distribute 7 points among them, with a maximum of 3 in any Virtue at character creation.

Set Attributes

Hunters use standard Storyteller attributes — nine traits in three categories. Prioritize categories (7/5/3 dots), then distribute within each category.

Physical

  • Strength — Raw muscle power, carrying capacity, melee damage
  • Dexterity — Speed, agility, reflexes, coordination, aim
  • Stamina — Endurance, toughness, resistance to damage and fatigue

Social

  • Charisma — Force of personality, charm, natural leadership
  • Manipulation — Persuasion, deception, getting what you want
  • Appearance — Physical attractiveness, first impressions, presence

Mental

  • Perception — Awareness, noticing details, reading situations
  • Intelligence — Raw mental power, analysis, problem-solving
  • Wits — Quick thinking, reaction time, street smarts

Conviction & Willpower

CONVICTION
Conviction is your fuel. It powers Edges and sustains your Hunt. You start with your highest Virtue in Conviction. Regain it by acting according to your Creed. Lose it by betraying your principles.

Willpower

  • Starting Willpower = 3
  • Spend to resist supernatural powers, push through pain, or activate certain Edges
  • Regain slowly through rest or achieving personal goals
  • Different from Conviction — Willpower is personal strength, Conviction is divine purpose
Running out of Conviction is dangerous. Without it, you're just a normal person who sees monsters. You can't activate Edges. You can't defend yourself. And the monsters know when you're weak.

How Rolling Works

Hunter uses the Storyteller System — roll d10s, count successes. Simple. Brutal. Deadly.

D10
Build Your Pool
Attribute + Ability = number of d10s you roll. Example: Dexterity 3 + Firearms 2 = roll 5 dice.
6+
Count Successes
Every die showing 6 or higher = 1 success. Difficulty usually 6. More successes = better result.
10
Roll Again
Roll a 10? That's 1 success AND you roll it again. 10s keep adding up. Miracles happen.
1
Botch Risk
Roll NO successes AND any 1s? That's a botch. Critical failure. Something goes very wrong.

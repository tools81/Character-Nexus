KIDS ON BIKES

(secret club adventures — summer 1987)
System: Collaborative d4/d6/d8/d10/d12/d20
Made By: Renegade Game Studios
Setting: Small town America, 1980s nostalgia
Remember: Bring flashlights!
You're a kid in a small town. Maybe it's the 1980s. Maybe it's today. Strange things are happening — mysteries nobody believes, adventures nobody knows about. Adults don't listen. Teachers don't understand. But you and your friends? You know something's up. You've got bikes, flashlights, and each other. That's all you need. Time to solve mysteries, find adventure, and be home before dark.

Pick Your Age

How old are you? Your age determines your stats and what you're good at. Younger kids are more creative and brave. Older kids are stronger and smarter.

AGES 11-12
The Younger Kid
You're the youngest in the group. Adults underestimate you. That's your advantage. You notice things others miss and you're not afraid to try crazy stuff.
Stats: d12 Guts, d10 Charm, d8 Smarts, d6 Brawn, d4 Fight, d4 Flight
AGES 13-14
The Middle Kid
You're right in the middle — not too young, not too old. You balance everything. You're adaptable and can handle most situations.
Stats: d12 Charm, d10 Smarts, d8 Guts, d6 Fight, d4 Brawn, d4 Flight
AGES 15-16
The Older Kid
You're the oldest (or close to it). More mature, stronger, and people actually listen to you sometimes. You can drive (maybe), and you've got responsibilities.
Stats: d12 Smarts, d10 Brawn, d8 Flight, d6 Charm, d4 Guts, d4 Fight
AGES 17-18
The Almost-Adult
You're on the edge of adulthood. You can actually drive. You work part-time. You're responsible for stuff. But you still remember being a kid.
Stats: d12 Brawn, d10 Flight, d8 Smarts, d6 Fight, d4 Charm, d4 Guts
→ IMPORTANT! Your age gives you different-sized dice for each stat. Bigger dice = better at that thing! A d12 is way better than a d4. You'll roll these dice when you try stuff.

Your Stats Explained

Every kid has six stats. Each stat uses a different die based on your age. When you try something, you roll the appropriate die!

Brawn
Physical strength, athletics, toughness
d4-d12
Charm
Talking to people, lying, being likeable
d4-d12
Fight
Standing your ground, not backing down
d4-d12
Flight
Running away, avoiding danger, escaping
d4-d12
Guts
Bravery, willpower, facing fear
d4-d12
Smarts
Intelligence, problem-solving, knowledge
d4-d12

Pick Your Strengths

Choose two Strengths — things you're really good at. When you use a Strength, you roll with advantage (roll twice, keep the better result)!

Athletic
You're good at sports, running, climbing, physical stuff. You're in shape!
Bookworm
You read everything. You know random facts. You're the encyclopedia of the group.
Charming
People like you. You can talk your way out of (or into) anything. Natural charisma!
Creative
You think outside the box. Art, music, weird solutions — you've got imagination!
Loyal
You never abandon your friends. Ever. They can count on you no matter what.
Mechanical
You fix things. Bikes, radios, whatever. You understand how stuff works.
Observant
You notice details others miss. Clues, lies, hidden stuff — you see it.
Rebellious
You break rules, take risks, and question authority. Sometimes that's exactly what's needed.

Pick Your Flaw

Choose one Flaw — something that makes your life harder. When your Flaw comes up, you roll with disadvantage (roll twice, keep the worse result). But flaws make you interesting!

Clumsy
You trip, drop things, knock stuff over. Your coordination isn't great.
Coward
You scare easily. Loud noises, dark places, strange things — you want to run!
Impulsive
You act before thinking. This gets you (and your friends) into trouble constantly.
Liar
You lie a lot. Sometimes you can't help it. People are starting to notice.
Outcast
You don't fit in. Other kids avoid you. Social situations are hard.
Sheltered
Your parents are super protective. You don't know much about the "real world."
Short-Tempered
You get angry fast. You say things you regret. You might throw punches.
Weak
You're not physically strong. Heavy things, long runs, fights — you struggle.

How to Roll Dice

When you try something, the GM (Game Master) tells you which stat to roll. Roll that die. Higher is better! You're trying to beat a target number.

Simple version:

  • Easy stuff: Roll 3+ to succeed
  • Normal stuff: Roll 7+ to succeed
  • Hard stuff: Roll 11+ to succeed
  • Really hard stuff: Roll 15+ to succeed

Your Strengths help (roll twice, keep better). Your Flaw hurts (roll twice, keep worse). That's it!

Adversity Tokens

When you fail a roll, you get an Adversity Token. Save them up! You can spend tokens to:

  • Re-roll a failed roll (spend 1 token)
  • Help a friend — give them +1 to their roll (spend 1 token)
  • Flashback — declare you brought something useful (spend 2 tokens)
  • Coincidence — make something convenient happen (spend 3 tokens)

Failing isn't the end — it gives you tokens to be awesome later!

Your Stuff

What do you have? You're a kid, so nothing too fancy. But you've got the essentials!

Everyone Has:

  • Your bike — freedom, transportation, escape
  • Flashlight — for dark places (bring extra batteries!)
  • Backpack — holds all your stuff
  • $5-20 — allowance money or babysitting cash

Pick 2-3 Extra Things:

  • Walkie-talkie (talk to friends from far away)
  • First aid kit (Band-Aids and stuff)
  • Camera (take pictures of evidence!)
  • Baseball bat (for "protection")
  • Rope (always useful)
  • Comic books (inspiration and comfort)
  • Snacks (granola bars, candy)
  • Map of town (hand-drawn is fine)

Who Are You?

Now the important stuff. Who is your character? Think about:

  • What's your name? (nickname too!)
  • What do you look like? (clothes, hair, anything special?)
  • Where do you live? (big house? apartment? trailer park?)
  • What's your family like? (siblings? strict parents? divorced?)
  • What's your deal at school? (popular? nerd? troublemaker?)
  • What do you want? (fit in? be brave? solve mysteries?)
  • What scares you? (the dark? bullies? being alone?)
  • Why do you hang out with this group?

Your Friends

You're not alone! You've got your crew. Define your relationships with the other kids in the group.

For Each Friend, Decide:

  • How did you meet? (school? neighbors? forced together?)
  • Why do you like them? (what's good about them?)
  • What annoys you about them? (nobody's perfect)
  • What secret do you share? (something only you two know)
→ Pro Tip! The best groups have some tension. Maybe you and another kid both like the same person. Maybe there's a rivalry. Maybe you're enemies who have to work together. Drama makes it fun!

You're Ready!

  • ☐ Age picked (determines your dice)
  • ☐ Stats assigned (different die for each)
  • ☐ Two Strengths chosen
  • ☐ One Flaw chosen
  • ☐ Name and appearance described
  • ☐ Background established
  • ☐ Equipment selected
  • ☐ Relationships with friends defined
  • ☐ Bike ready to ride
  • ☐ Flashlight has batteries
  • ☐ Ready for adventure!
That's it! You're ready to ride your bike into adventure. Remember: be home before dark (but you probably won't be). Adults won't believe what you find. Teachers will tell you to focus on homework. Parents will ground you if they catch you sneaking out. But mysteries don't solve themselves. Strange things are happening in your town. And you and your friends? You're the only ones who can stop them. Now grab your flashlight and let's go!

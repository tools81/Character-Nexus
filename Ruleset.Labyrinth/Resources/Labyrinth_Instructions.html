THE Labyrinth

Adventure Game
BASED ON: Jim Henson's Labyrinth (1986)
SYSTEM: River Horse Games / 2d6
SETTING: The Goblin King's Labyrinth
WARNING: Nothing is as it seems
Through Dangers Untold
Welcome to the Labyrinth — a shifting maze of impossible architecture, talking doors, and creatures most peculiar. You are a dreamer, pulled into this strange realm by the Goblin King. Perhaps you spoke the words ("I wish the goblins would take you away... right now!"). Perhaps you simply wandered too far into the fantastical. Now you must navigate the Labyrinth, solve its riddles, face its denizens, and reach the castle at the center before time runs out. Remember: Nothing here is quite what it seems.

Determine Your Abilities

Every character has four Abilities rated from 0 to 6. Distribute 12 points among them, with a maximum of 6 in any single Ability.

Ability Description
Physical Strength, agility, athletics, physical actions
Mental Intelligence, reasoning, problem-solving, memory
Social Charm, persuasion, empathy, social interaction
Special Unusual talents, magic sensitivity, artistic gifts
Point-Buy System: You have 12 points to spend. Each point increases an Ability by 1. You can have:

• Balanced: 3 / 3 / 3 / 3 (decent at everything)
• Specialist: 6 / 3 / 2 / 1 (expert in one area)
• Versatile: 4 / 4 / 2 / 2 (good at two things)

Choose based on how you want to approach challenges!

Select Your Skills

Skills represent specific areas of expertise. Choose three Skills that reflect your character's talents and background.

Sample Skills

  • Acting: Pretend, disguise, perform convincingly
  • Athletics: Run, jump, climb, physical feats
  • Craft: Make things, repair objects, artistic creation
  • Insight: Read people, detect lies, understand motives
  • Intimidation: Threaten, frighten, assert dominance
  • Investigation: Find clues, solve puzzles, research
  • Lore: Knowledge of stories, myths, fairy tales
  • Music: Play instruments, sing, use sound
  • Nature: Understand plants, animals, natural world
  • Perception: Notice details, spot hidden things
  • Persuasion: Convince, negotiate, make friends
  • Stealth: Sneak, hide, move quietly

Define Your Quirk

Every character has a Quirk — something unusual, strange, or defining about them. This is what makes you you.

Example Quirks "Always quotes stories and books" — You see life through narratives
"Talks to inanimate objects" — And sometimes they answer
"Compulsively honest" — Cannot tell a lie (even when it helps)
"Obsessed with riddles" — You must solve every puzzle
"Theatrical to a fault" — Everything deserves dramatic flair
"Sees magic everywhere" — The mundane world bores you
"Fiercely protective of the small" — Underdogs are your cause
"Never backs down" — Stubbornness is your superpower

Establish Your Ties

Ties are your connections to the waking world — people, places, or things you care about. They anchor you and give you reason to escape the Labyrinth.

Creating Ties

  • Person: A sibling, parent, friend, or someone you must return to
  • Place: Home, a special location, somewhere you belong
  • Treasure: An object of deep personal meaning
  • Dream: An ambition or goal in the waking world

Choose at least two Ties. These ground you and help you resist the Labyrinth's temptations.

Rolling the Dice

When you attempt a challenging action, roll 2d6 and add your Ability + Skill (if applicable). Compare to a Difficulty Number.

Roll 2d6 + Ability + Skill vs. Difficulty

Difficulty 6: Easy
Difficulty 9: Moderate
Difficulty 12: Hard
Difficulty 15+: Very Hard

Meet or exceed the Difficulty = Success!
Rolling double 1s = Complication (something goes wrong)
Rolling double 6s = Triumph (something extra happens)
Example Roll You're trying to climb a wall of hands (Physical challenge).
Your Physical is 4. You have Athletics skill (+1).
Difficulty is 10.

You roll: 3 + 5 = 8, plus your Physical 4 + Athletics 1 = 13 total
Success! You climb the wall!

The Thirteen Hour Clock

TIME IS RUNNING OUT
The Goblin King gave you thirteen hours to solve the Labyrinth. Time moves strangely here — sometimes fast, sometimes slow. The Game Master tracks Hour Tokens. When thirteen hours pass, the game ends. Will you reach the castle in time?

Spending Time

  • Exploring an area: 1 hour
  • Solving a major puzzle: 1-2 hours
  • Combat or confrontation: 1 hour
  • Resting or recovering: 2 hours
  • Getting lost or distracted: Variable (GM decides)

Navigating the Otherworld

The Labyrinth operates on fairy tale logic. Rules you expect don't always apply. Here's what you should know:

1. Nothing Is As It Seems: Doors lie. Paths loop. "Up" might be "down."
2. Words Have Power: Be careful what you wish for. Speak precisely.
3. Deals Are Binding: If you agree to something, you're bound to it.
4. Kindness Matters: Help given is often returned.
5. Riddles Must Be Answered: The Labyrinth loves games.
6. Time Is Fluid: Minutes might be hours. Hours might be seconds.
7. The Goblin King Watches: Always.

Finding Companions

You are not alone. The Labyrinth is full of strange beings — some helpful, some treacherous, all peculiar.

Potential Allies

  • Worm: Small, friendly, offers unexpected wisdom and shelter
  • Dwarf: Grumpy but loyal, knows secret paths
  • Goblin (reformed): Turncoat from the Goblin King's court
  • Fairy: Mischievous but can be bargained with
  • Stone Guardian: Ancient, bound by oaths, speaks in riddles
  • Fellow Dreamer: Another human lost in the maze

Companions provide assistance, but they have their own agendas. Trust carefully.

The Castle Beyond the Goblin City

Your Goal Awaits
At the center of the Labyrinth stands the Goblin King's castle. There, you will face him — charming, dangerous, offering everything you think you want. He will tempt you with dreams made real. He will offer to make you forget your troubles. He will show you a world where you never have to grow up.

But remember your Ties. Remember who you are. Remember what you came for.

You have no power over me.

That is how you win.

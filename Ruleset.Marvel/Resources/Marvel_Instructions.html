Assemble Your Champion
The Marvel Multiverse RPG uses the d616 System ‚Äî roll 3d6 (one being the special Marvel die) and add your relevant stat. Creating a hero is a narrative and mechanical process that produces a character unique to the Marvel universe.

Step 1Choose Your Origin

Your origin describes how you gained your powers and shapes the starting framework of your hero. It grants you an Origin Trait and informs your backstory.

Core Origins
  • Altered Human: Changed by accident, experiment, or cosmic event (Spider-Man, Hulk, Captain America)
  • High-Tech: Power comes from advanced technology you build or wear (Iron Man, War Machine)
  • Mutant: Born with the X-gene granting you superhuman abilities (X-Men, Magneto)
  • Supernatural: Powers granted by magic, mysticism, or divine forces (Doctor Strange, Thor)
  • Inhuman: Transformed by Terrigenesis into a super-powered being
  • Alien: Non-human heritage provides extraordinary abilities (Captain Marvel, Groot)
  • Training: Peak-human mastery through intense discipline (Hawkeye, Black Widow)

Step 3Assign Your Stats

Every hero has six core stats. Distribute your available points (determined by Rank) among them. Each stat starts at 0 and represents your hero's core capabilities.

Might
Physical strength, toughness, and endurance
Agility
Speed, reflexes, and physical coordination
Resilience
Resistance to damage, disease, and hardship
Vigilance
Perception, awareness, and tactical thinking
Ego
Mental strength, willpower, and personality force
Logic
Intelligence, problem-solving, and technical skill

The maximum number of points you may put in any single stat depends on your Rank. Higher Rank heroes can invest more in any one area.

Step 4Select Your Powers

Powers are your hero's special abilities beyond normal human capacity. Each power has its own rules and can be upgraded over time. Powers have both a base effect and can be enhanced with Power Sets.

Power Sets

Powers are grouped into thematic sets. Choose power sets that match your origin and concept:

Basic Hand-to-Hand Basic Ranged Elemental Control Energy Control Flight Healing Factor Illusion Intellect Magic Melee Weapons Phasing Psychic Ranged Weapons Shield Bearer Size Changing Spider-Powers Super-Speed Super-Strength Telekinesis Teleportation Weather Control

Purchasing Powers

Powers are purchased with your Rank points. Each power has a point cost. You must purchase the prerequisite powers in a set before advancing to higher-tier abilities.

  • All heroes start with at least one power set from their origin
  • You may purchase powers from multiple sets if your concept warrants it
  • Powers can be Signature Powers ‚Äî especially iconic abilities ‚Äî which improve on Fantastic successes

Step 5Choose Your Traits

Traits are descriptive tags that reflect your hero's skills, knowledge, and experience. They grant bonus dice or narrative advantages in relevant situations.

Trait Categories

  • Occupation Traits: "Scientist," "Soldier," "Street Fighter," "Spy," "Journalist" ‚Äî represent professional background
  • Knowledge Traits: "History of Asgard," "Criminal Underworld," "S.H.I.E.L.D. Protocols" ‚Äî specialized information
  • Personality Traits: "Natural Leader," "Never Gives Up," "Wisecracker" ‚Äî character qualities that aid in relevant situations
  • Reputation Traits: "Feared by Criminals," "Trusted Avenger," "Famous Scientist" ‚Äî how the world perceives your hero
üí° Narrator's Tip Traits work best when they're specific enough to be meaningful but broad enough to apply in multiple situations. A trait should feel like something your hero would say about themselves.

Defense & Damage

  • Melee Defense: 10 + Agility
  • Ranged Defense: 10 + Vigilance
  • Melee Damage Bonus: Based on Might rank

Step 8Define Your Tags

Tags are descriptive labels that help the Narrator and players understand what your hero is all about. They have no direct mechanical effect but guide roleplay and story decisions.

  • Distinction: A core personality quality ("Noble," "Reckless," "Driven by Guilt")
  • Relationship: A key connection ("Mentor: Captain America," "Rival: Ultron," "Partner: Black Widow")
  • Secret Identity: Do you have a civilian identity you protect?
  • Motivation: What drives your hero? ("Protect the innocent," "Prove mutants deserve respect," "Atone for past failures")

Step 9Choose Your Equipment

Some heroes rely on gadgets, weapons, and gear. Equipment that is integral to your hero's identity can be treated as powers from a High-Tech or Ranged/Melee Weapons power set.

  • Iconic items (Iron Man's armor, Thor's hammer) are purchased as part of your power set
  • Standard gear (uniforms, vehicles, communications) is handled narratively
  • Tech heroes may list specific gadgets with costs from their point budget
  • Items can have their own Rank for damage or defense purposes

Step 10Write Your Backstory

Bring your hero fully to life by answering these questions with your Narrator:

  • The Origin Moment: How did you gain your powers? What happened that day?
  • The Inciting Incident: What drove you to become a hero rather than use your abilities for personal gain ‚Äî or do nothing at all?
  • The Greatest Failure: What have you done (or failed to do) that still haunts you?
  • The Secret: What does your hero hide from the world, or from themselves?
  • The Relationship: Who is the most important person in your life, and what would you sacrifice to protect them?
  • The Villain: Who is your nemesis, and why is it personal?

Final Details

  • Hero Name: What the world calls you
  • Real Name: (if you have a secret identity)
  • Appearance: Costume colors, build, distinctive features
  • Base of Operations: New York rooftops? Wakandan palace? Deep space?

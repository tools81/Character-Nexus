Magic & Resonance

If your Priority gives you Magic or Resonance, you're special. Magicians cast spells. Adepts have superhuman physical abilities. Technomancers hack with their minds.

Awakened Types

  • Magician: Full spellcaster. Learn spells, summon spirits, see astral space.
  • Adept: Physical magic. Channel magic into combat abilities, enhanced senses, or incredible reflexes.
  • Aspected Magician: Limited magic (sorcery OR conjuring, not both).
  • Aware: Can perceive magic/astral but can't cast spells.

Technomancers

You interface with the Matrix through your mind — no cyberdeck needed. Compile sprites (digital spirits), thread complex forms (living programs), and see the digital world in ways no hacker can. Corps fear and hunt you.

Purchase Equipment

Spend your Resources from Priority. Buy weapons, armor, cyberware, vehicles, fake IDs, commlinks — everything you need to survive.

Essential Gear

  • Commlink: Your phone/computer. Everyone has one. (¥1,000-50,000+)
  • Fake SIN: Fake identity. Mandatory. Rating 4+ recommended. (¥10,000+)
  • Armor: Armor jacket, full body suit, or armored clothing. (¥1,000-15,000)
  • Weapon: Pistol, SMG, rifle, or melee. Depends on role. (¥500-25,000)
  • Lifestyle: Where you live. Street (free) to Luxury (¥100,000/month).

Cyberware

Augmentations replace body parts with chrome and circuits. They're powerful but cost Essence (your humanity/magic potential). Too much and you can't cast spells — or worse.

  • Cybereyes: Enhanced vision, low-light, thermographic (¥2,000+)
  • Cyberarms: Superhuman strength, weapon mounts (¥15,000+)
  • Wired Reflexes: Enhanced speed, more actions (¥30,000+)
  • Datajack: Direct neural interface for Matrix access (¥1,000)

Understanding Edge

EDGE
Edge is everything. It's luck, fate, heroism. Spend Edge to: re-roll dice, ignore wounds, negate glitches, push the odds, or pull off miracles. It resets every session. When you run out, you're playing on hard mode. Use it or lose it.

How to Spend Edge

  • Before Roll: Add Edge rating to dice pool
  • After Roll: Re-roll failures
  • Burn Edge: One permanent point for auto-success or cheat death
  • Push the Limit: Ignore wound penalties, limits, or other restrictions

Roll Mechanics

Shadowrun uses dice pools. Roll Attribute + Skill in d6s. Each die showing 5 or 6 is a success ("hit"). You need hits to beat thresholds or opposed rolls.

> CALCULATING SUCCESS PROBABILITY... ROLLING: Attribute + Skill = Dice Pool SUCCESS: Each 5 or 6 = 1 Hit THRESHOLD: Number of hits needed to succeed EXAMPLE: Shooting a guard (Agility 5 + Firearms 4 = 9 dice) Roll: 1, 2, 5, 5, 6, 3, 4, 6, 5 = 5 Hits Guard Defends: Reaction 4 + Intuition 3 = 7 dice = 3 Hits You win by 2 hits. Guard is hit. GLITCHES: If more than half dice show 1s • Glitch = Bad luck, complications • Critical Glitch (0 hits + glitch) = Catastrophic failure > SYSTEM READY

Establish Contacts

Contacts are people who help you. Fixers, gang members, corporate insiders, hackers — they provide info, gear, or jobs. You get (Charisma + Loyalty) points to build contacts.

Contact Stats

  • Connection: How well-connected they are (1-6)
  • Loyalty: How much they like you (1-6)
  • Cost: Connection + Loyalty = total points spent

Example Contacts

  • Fixer (Connection 4, Loyalty 2): Gets you gear and jobs
  • Street Doc (Connection 2, Loyalty 3): Patches you up no questions asked
  • Corp Exec (Connection 5, Loyalty 1): Insider info on megacorps
  • Decker Friend (Connection 2, Loyalty 4): Matrix support, loyal as hell

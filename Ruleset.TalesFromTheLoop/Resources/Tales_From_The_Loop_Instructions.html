⬡ CASE FILE — KID INVESTIGATOR

TALES FROM THE LOOP

"Strange things happen near the Loop. Kids see things adults don't believe.
This is how you become one of those kids."
SYSTEM: Year Zero Engine (d6 Dice Pool)
PUBLISHER: Free League Publishing
SETTING: 1980s Alternate Reality / Sweden or USA
THEMES: Mystery, Wonder, Coming of Age
You're a kid — 10 to 15 years old. You live in a world where impossible machines exist, strange phenomena happen near the Loop (a massive particle accelerator), and adults are too busy to notice. You ride your bike to school. You have homework. You argue with your parents. But you also investigate mysteries, encounter robots in the woods, and see things that defy explanation. You can't die in this game — the worst that happens is you go home. But the mysteries? Those are real.

Determine Luck

Every kid has Luck — points you can spend to modify dice rolls or avoid bad situations. Your starting Luck equals 15 minus your age.

Younger kids are luckier:

  • Age 10 = 5 Luck
  • Age 12 = 3 Luck
  • Age 15 = 0 Luck

Spend 1 Luck to: re-roll a failed roll, turn a failure into a success, avoid a Condition, or make something convenient happen. Luck recovers slowly (GM decides when).

Choose Iconic Item

Your Type suggests an Iconic Item — something meaningful to you. It might be useful, sentimental, or both. This item gives you +1 die when it's relevant to a roll.

Example Iconic Items

  • Your Bike: Freedom, mobility, escape
  • Polaroid Camera: Evidence, documentation, memories
  • Walkie-Talkie: Communication with friends
  • Flashlight: Exploring dark places
  • Comic Book Collection: Inspiration, knowledge of tropes
  • Dad's Toolbox: Fixing things, looking capable
  • Lucky Charm: Confidence, superstition
  • Mixtape: Emotional connection, sharing with friends

Define Relationships

Who matters to you? Every kid has Anchors — people who ground you emotionally. Choose at least two.

Types of Anchors

  • Parent/Guardian: Mom, Dad, Grandparent — complicated but important
  • Sibling: Annoying but you'd protect them
  • Best Friend: Someone who gets you
  • Crush: That person you think about constantly
  • Mentor: Teacher, coach, older kid who inspires you
  • Pet: Dog, cat, or other companion who's always there

Anchors help you recover from Conditions — talking to them, being with them, helps you heal.

Rolling Dice

When you try something risky, roll d6s equal to Attribute + Skill. Each die showing 6 is a success. You usually need 1 success to succeed.

[ DICE POOL EXAMPLE ] You're trying to climb a fence (Move skill, Body attribute). You have Body 3 and Move 2. Roll 5d6. You roll: 2, 4, 6, 6, 3. Two sixes = two successes! You climb the fence easily.

Results

  • 1+ Success: You succeed! Extra successes might mean you do it faster/better/quieter.
  • 0 Success: You fail. Something goes wrong or you can't do it.
  • Push the Roll: If you fail, you can re-roll all non-6s BUT you take a Condition (get hurt, scared, upset, or exhausted).

Conditions & Stress

CONDITIONS
Track five Conditions: Upset, Scared, Exhausted, Injured, Broken.
When you mark all 5, you can't continue — you go home. You're safe, but out of the scene.

What Each Condition Means

  • Upset: Angry, sad, frustrated. Emotional turmoil.
  • Scared: Terrified, anxious, panicking.
  • Exhausted: Tired, drained, pushing your limits.
  • Injured: Hurt, bruised, bleeding.
  • Broken: Completely overwhelmed — physically or emotionally.

Recover Conditions by: resting at home, talking to an Anchor, or having a meaningful scene with someone who cares.

[ IMPORTANT ] You can't die. The worst that happens is you're too hurt/scared/upset to continue and you go home. Adults might find out. You might be grounded. But you're safe. Kids don't die in Tales From the Loop — but they do face consequences.

Choose Your Problem

Every kid has a Problem — something wrong in your life. This grounds you in reality and gives you depth. Pick one or create your own.

  • Your parents are getting divorced
  • You're being bullied at school
  • Your family is struggling financially
  • You have a sick relative
  • You moved to town recently and don't have friends
  • Your parent works at the Loop and is never home
  • You're dealing with grief from a loss
  • You feel like an outsider everywhere
  • You're questioning your identity
  • You have a learning disability teachers don't understand

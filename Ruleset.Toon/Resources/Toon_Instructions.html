TOON! Cartoon RPG

Where Anything Can Happen (And Usually Does!)
STUDIO: Steve Jackson Games
ANIMATION TYPE: Classic Cartoon Comedy
RATING: Silly for All Ages
REQUIRED: Six-Sided Dice, Sense of Humor, Anvils
Welcome to Toon! You're about to become a cartoon character in a world where gravity is optional, anvils fall from nowhere, and getting flattened by a steamroller just means you're temporarily two-dimensional. You can't die (just get knocked out for a scene), physics are suggestions, and the only limit is your imagination (and maybe the GM's patience). Ready to cause some cartoon chaos? Let's go!

Pick Your Species

What kind of cartoon are you? A talking animal? A robot? A human? In Toon, your Species is just the starting point for silliness!

🐰
Funny Animal
Classic cartoon critter! You're a talking, walking animal doing human things. Bugs Bunny, Mickey Mouse, Donald Duck — you get the idea. Walk on two legs, wear clothes, cause mayhem.
Natural Skills: Jump Good, Smell Stuff
👤
Human
Regular person in a cartoon world! You're either the straight man trying to make sense of chaos, or you're just as wacky as everyone else. Either way, physics still doesn't apply to you.
Natural Skills: Drive Crazy, Fix Anything
🤖
Robot
Mechanical mayhem! Springs pop out when you're surprised. Gears turn visibly. You can detach limbs and keep going. Beep boop, you're a walking disaster (in the best way).
Natural Skills: Compute Stuff, Shock People
👻
Supernatural
Ghost, vampire, monster, alien — whatever weird thing you want! You've got spooky powers but you're still fundamentally silly. Think Casper, not horror movie.
Natural Skills: Spook People, Walk Through Walls
🎪
Anything Else!
Be creative! Animated object? Living food? Sentient vehicle? In Toon, if you can imagine it, you can play it. Just make sure it's funny!
Natural Skills: Whatever Makes Sense!

Roll Your Stats

Every Toon has four stats. Roll 3d6 for each one, or assign these numbers: 12, 10, 9, 7. Higher is better!

Muscle
How strong you are! Lift pianos, punch through walls, carry ridiculous loads.
3-18
Zip
How fast you are! Run like the wind, dodge falling anvils, zip around corners.
3-18
Smarts
How clever you are! Come up with plans, solve puzzles, outsmart opponents.
3-18
Chutzpah
How bold you are! Talk your way out of trouble, resist fear, stay confident when flattened.
3-18

Choose Your Skills

Pick four Skills at level 6, plus two Skills at level 3. Skills let you do specific things better. Want more skills? Lower existing ones to get new ones at level 3!

Drive Crazy
Pilot any vehicle at breakneck speed! Cars, planes, rocket ships, rollercoasters — you can drive 'em all (terribly).
Dodge
Get out of the way of danger! Anvils, pianos, giant boulders, explosions — nope! Not today!
Fight
Hit things! Punch, kick, bonk with mallets, drop anvils on heads. Combat, cartoon style.
Fast-Talk
Talk your way out of anything! Con people, make deals, distract guards, sell snake oil.
Jump Good
Leap tall buildings! Jump over chasms! Bounce like a superball! Fall slowly if you don't look down!
Identify Dangerous Things
Spot traps, notice TNT, realize that's not a real tunnel painted on the wall. Knowledge saves you!
Sleight of Hand
Pick pockets, do magic tricks, pull impossibly large items from your pockets (where do they all fit?).
Climb Up Walls
Scramble up anything! Buildings, trees, cliffs — gravity only works if you think about it.
See in the Dark
Navigate darkness! Your eyes glow comically. You might pull out a flashlight from nowhere.
Bamboozle
Confuse people with nonsense! Distract, mislead, overwhelm with gibberish. Works surprisingly often!
💭 DIRECTOR'S NOTE You can make up new skills! "Juggle Chainsaws"? Sure! "Cook Terrible Food"? Absolutely! "Annoy People"? Classic! Just get your GM's okay and you're good to go!

Set Your Beliefs

Every Toon has Beliefs — things your character thinks are true. These shape how you play. Pick two or three!

  • "I'm the smartest character here!" (Even if you're not)
  • "Violence solves everything!" (Hit it with a mallet)
  • "I'm irresistible!" (To everyone's confusion)
  • "Rules don't apply to me!" (Gravity included)
  • "I can talk my way out of anything!" (Usually true in Toon)
  • "Nobody appreciates my genius!" (They really don't)
  • "If it can go wrong, it will!" (Murphy's Law: Cartoon Edition)
  • "There's always a better way!" (Even if it's more complicated)

Get Plot Points

You start with 3 Plot Points. Spend them to:

PLOT POINTS!
• Re-roll a failed roll
• Do something amazing
• Get exactly the item you need (pull it from nowhere)
• Survive something that should definitely knock you out
Gain more Plot Points by being funny, entertaining everyone, or doing ridiculous things!

How to Roll

When you try something tricky, roll 1d6. Add your Stat and Skill. Compare to the Difficulty Number (set by the GM, usually 7-14).

Roll 1d6 + Stat + Skill vs. Difficulty

  • Equal or Higher: You succeed!
  • Lower: You fail (often hilariously)
  • Roll a 6: Critical success! Add +2 to your total!
  • Roll a 1: Critical failure! Subtract 2 from your total (and something funny happens)!

Example Difficulties

  • 7: Easy (jump over a log)
  • 9: Average (dodge a thrown pie)
  • 11: Hard (drive through a busy street backwards)
  • 14: Very Hard (catch a falling anvil)
  • 18+: Nearly Impossible (talk the villain into reforming)

Getting Hit

When something hits you (anvil, explosion, fist), you take damage. Each hit reduces your Hit Points (start at your Muscle + Chutzpah).

💭 IMPORTANT! You can't die in Toon! When you run out of Hit Points, you're knocked out until the next scene. You come back totally fine, no permanent damage. Cartoon logic!

Falling Down & Getting Up

When knocked out:

  • You're out for the rest of the scene
  • You wake up with full Hit Points next scene
  • You might have funny cartoon injuries (stars circling your head, accordion body, etc.) but they don't affect gameplay
  • You're totally fine and ready to get flattened again!

Your Stuff

You can carry whatever makes sense for your character! In Toon, you can pull things from hammerspace (nowhere) if it's funny.

Common Cartoon Items

  • Mallet: For bonking people. Does 1d6 damage.
  • Portable Hole: Put it on walls to make a tunnel! Put it on the ground to make a pit!
  • ACME Product: Whatever you need, ACME makes it (and it probably doesn't work right).
  • Rocket Skates: Super fast! Also super dangerous!
  • Giant Magnet: Attracts metal things (including bullets flying at you).
  • Disguise Kit: Look like anyone! Usually poorly, but it works anyway.
  • TNT: For when you need an explosion. Comes with convenient fuse.
  • Rope: Infinite length. Somehow fits in your pocket.

Add Personality

What makes your Toon you? Think about:

  • What's your catchphrase? ("What's up, Doc?" / "Sufferin' succotash!")
  • What's your goal? (Catch that rabbit / Get rich / Be famous / Cause chaos)
  • What always goes wrong for you? (Plans backfire / Gravity kicks in at the worst time)
  • Who's your nemesis? (That pesky rabbit / The mean cat / The grumpy cop)
  • What makes you laugh? (Pranks / Slapstick / Wordplay / Everything)

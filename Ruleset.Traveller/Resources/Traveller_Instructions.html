TRAVELLER

Mongoose Publishing — Second Edition
System: 2D6 + Modifiers (Roll High) Publisher: Mongoose Publishing Setting: Third Imperium / OTU / Hard Science Fiction Era: Year 1105 Imperial Calendar
You are a traveller — spacer, trader, mercenary, scout, or drifter navigating the vast expanse between the stars. The Third Imperium spans thousands of worlds connected by jump drives. You've survived your career (or careers), accumulated skills, and maybe a few scars. Character creation simulates your life before the game begins. Warning: Your character can die during generation. Roll poorly, make bad choices, and you'll be rolling up a new character. That's Traveller.

Each Characteristic provides a Dice Modifier (DM) based on its value:

  • 0-1: DM-2 // 2: DM-1 // 3-5: DM-1 // 6-8: DM+0
  • 9-11: DM+1 // 12-14: DM+2 // 15+: DM+3

These DMs apply to skill checks. High characteristics make you better at related tasks.

Determine Background

Before your career, you had a Background. This grants your first skill. Roll on the Background Skills table or choose based on your homeworld.

Common Background Skills

  • Admin — Bureaucracy, record-keeping
  • Art — Creative expression
  • Athletics — Physical activity, sports
  • Carouse — Social drinking, partying, networking
  • Drive — Operating ground vehicles
  • Science — Academic knowledge (pick specialty)
  • Streetwise — Urban survival, black market dealings
  • Survival — Wilderness survival (pick environment)

Serve Terms

Each Term is 4 years of service. During a term, you roll for survival, advancement, skills, and events. You can leave after any term or continue serving.

CAREER TERM
1 TERM = 4 YEARS
Each term ages you by 4 years and may grant skills, rank, and benefits.
Fail a survival roll and you may be injured, killed, or forced out.

Term Sequence

  1. Survival Roll: Roll 2D6 + DMs. Meet target or face consequences (injury/death table)
  2. Skill Check: Choose a skill table and roll for a skill
  3. Event Roll: Roll on the Events table for your career
  4. Advancement Roll: Roll to increase rank (if eligible)
  5. Aging Check: If 34+ years old, roll on Aging table (characteristics may decline)
  6. Re-enlistment Decision: Continue for another term or muster out?
⬢ WARNING: CHARACTER DEATH If you fail a survival roll and roll poorly on the injury table, your character dies. This is intentional. Traveller characters are survivors — they've already beaten the odds just by making it to the game. Start over with a new concept if this happens.

Acquire Skills

Skills are learned abilities. You gain skills from Background, Career tables, and service. Skills have levels (0-4+). Higher levels mean greater competence.

Skill Category Example Skills
Combat Gun Combat, Melee, Heavy Weapons, Tactics
Spaceship Pilot, Engineer, Gunner, Astrogation, Sensors
Technical Mechanic, Electronics, Computer, Explosives, Medic
Vehicle Drive, Flyer, Seafarer (land/air/water vehicles)
Knowledge Admin, Advocate, Broker, Diplomat, Science
Physical Athletics, Stealth, Recon, Survival
Social Carouse, Deception, Persuade, Streetwise
  • Level 0: Basic familiarity — you know enough not to hurt yourself
  • Level 1: Trained professional
  • Level 2: Experienced veteran
  • Level 3: Expert
  • Level 4+: Master, recognized authority

Muster Out Benefits

When you leave service, you receive Benefits — one roll per term served, plus extra rolls for high rank. Roll on Cash or Material Benefits tables.

Cash Benefits

Credits (Cr) for immediate spending. You need money to survive, pay passage, and outfit yourself.

Material Benefits

  • Weapon: Military-grade sidearm or rifle
  • Armor: Combat armor, vacc suit, or protective gear
  • Ship Share: Partial ownership in a starship (accumulate shares to own one)
  • Scientific Equipment: Lab, sensors, or specialized tools
  • Contacts: Useful allies in various fields
  • Passage: Free high or middle passage tickets

The most valuable benefit is Ship Share. Accumulate enough and you own a starship — freedom, livelihood, and adventure.

Task Resolution System

To attempt a task, roll 2D6 + Skill Level + Characteristic DM. Compare to target number (usually 8+).

  • Simple (+6) // Easy (+4) // Routine (+2) // Average (+0)
  • Difficult (-2) // Very Difficult (-4) // Formidable (-6)

Example Check

Repairing ship's jump drive (Mechanic check, Average difficulty):
Roll 2D6 + Mechanic 2 + INT DM+1 = 2D6+3 vs. 8+
Result: 5+3 = 8. Success!

Handle Aging Effects

At age 34 and every 4 years after, you must roll on the Aging Table. Your physical characteristics may decline. This represents the toll of time and hard living.

⬢ AGING CRISIS Aging can cripple your character. High-term characters are skilled but physically declining. Medical technology (Anagathics) can slow aging for the wealthy. Plan your career length wisely.

Acquire Initial Gear

Use your mustered-out cash and benefits to equip yourself. You'll need basics plus career-appropriate gear.

Essential Equipment

  • Clothing: Standard or environment-appropriate (Cr 50-200)
  • Comms: Personal communicator (Cr 50)
  • Survival Gear: Breather mask, survival kit, tools
  • Weapon: If legal and you have one from benefits/training
  • Datapad: Computer access (Cr 100-1000)
  • Passage Tickets: Transportation between worlds

Career-Specific Gear

  • Navy/Marines: Vacc suit, weapon, rank insignia
  • Scout: Survey equipment, survival gear, ship (if lucky)
  • Merchant: Trader software, contacts, cargo manifest
  • Agent: Investigation tools, credentials, concealed weapon

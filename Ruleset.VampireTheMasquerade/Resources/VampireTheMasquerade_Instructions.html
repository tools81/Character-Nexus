Character Creation

Vampire: The Masquerade Fifth Edition

1Character Concept

Begin by envisioning your vampire's identity before and after the Embrace. Consider their mortal life, how they were turned, and who they've become in undeath.

  • Chronicle Tenets: Discuss with your Storyteller the core beliefs and rules of your chronicle
  • Predator Type: Choose how your vampire feeds (Alleycat, Bagger, Blood Leech, Cleaver, Consensualist, Farmer, Osiris, Sandman, Scene Queen, Siren, or Extortionist)
  • Ambition: Define a concrete goal your character seeks to achieve
  • Desire: Establish what truly drives your character emotionally

2Clan Selection

Your clan determines your vampiric lineage, inherent powers, and curse. Each of the thirteen clans offers a unique experience:

The Thirteen Clans: Brujah, Gangrel, Malkavian, Nosferatu, Toreador, Tremere, Ventrue, Caitiff, Banu Haqim, Hecata, Lasombra, The Ministry, Ravnos, Salubri, Tzimisce

Each clan grants you:

  • Two clan Disciplines at one dot each
  • A clan Compulsion (triggered when you roll a Bestial Failure)
  • A unique clan Bane (your vampiric curse)

3Attributes

Distribute dots among the nine Attributes, divided into three categories. You have three priority levels:

Physical

Strength • Dexterity • Stamina

Social

Charisma • Manipulation • Composure

Mental

Intelligence • Wits • Resolve

  • Primary (4 dots): Distribute among one category
  • Secondary (3 dots): Distribute among another category
  • Tertiary (2 dots): Distribute among the remaining category

All Attributes start at 1 dot. The dots you distribute are in addition to this base.

4Skills

Skills represent your character's learned abilities and training. Distribute dots using the same priority system as Attributes:

Physical Skills

Athletics, Brawl, Craft, Drive, Firearms, Larceny, Melee, Stealth, Survival

Social Skills

Animal Ken, Etiquette, Insight, Intimidation, Leadership, Performance, Persuasion, Streetwise, Subterfuge

Mental Skills

Academics, Awareness, Finance, Investigation, Medicine, Occult, Politics, Science, Technology

  • Primary (7 dots): Distribute among one category, max 3 dots per skill
  • Secondary (5 dots): Distribute among another category, max 3 dots per skill
  • Tertiary (3 dots): Distribute among the remaining category, max 3 dots per skill

Skill Specialties

Choose one specialty for each skill you have at 4 dots or higher. Specialties grant a +1 bonus to specific uses of that skill.

5Disciplines

Your Predator Type and Clan already grant you some Discipline dots. Now distribute 2 additional dots among any Disciplines you already possess (you cannot purchase new Disciplines at character creation without special permission).

Disciplines represent your vampiric supernatural powers. Common Disciplines include Animalism, Auspex, Blood Sorcery, Celerity, Dominate, Fortitude, Obfuscate, Oblivion, Potence, Presence, and Protean.

6Advantages

Backgrounds

Distribute 3 dots among Backgrounds, which represent your resources, allies, and connections:

  • Allies: Mortals who assist you
  • Contacts: Sources of information
  • Fame: Mortal renown
  • Haven: Quality and security of your sanctuary
  • Herd: Reliable feeding sources
  • Influence: Political or institutional power
  • Mask: Quality of your mortal facade
  • Resources: Wealth and assets
  • Retainers: Loyal servants
  • Status: Position in vampire society

Merits & Flaws

Spend up to 7 dots on Merits. You may take Flaws (worth 1-2 dots each) to gain additional Merit dots, up to a maximum of 2 additional dots.

7Convictions & Touchstones

Convictions

Create 3 Convictions — core moral beliefs your character holds. These are tested when you commit acts that violate them, potentially leading to Stains on your Humanity.

Touchstones

Establish 1 Touchstone per dot of Humanity. Touchstones are mortals (or rarely, other beings) who anchor you to your Humanity. Losing Touchstones can cause you to lose Humanity as well.

Work with your Storyteller to ensure your Convictions create meaningful dramatic tension and aren't simply ways to avoid all moral consequences.

8Humanity

All characters begin with Humanity 7 unless your Predator Type, clan, or specific character concept modifies this.

Humanity represents your connection to mortal ethics and your resistance to the Beast. It ranges from 10 (saint-like) to 0 (completely monstrous).

  • Higher Humanity makes it easier to resist frenzy and avoid Compulsions
  • Violating your Convictions causes Stains on your Humanity
  • Accumulating too many Stains can permanently reduce your Humanity

9Additional Traits

Blood Potency

All new vampires start at Blood Potency 1. This measures the strength of your vitae and increases over centuries or through diablerie.

Health & Willpower

  • Health: Equal to Stamina + 3
  • Willpower: Equal to Composure + Resolve

Generation

Most starting characters are 13th generation, though this may vary by chronicle. Thin-bloods are 14th or 15th generation.

10Finishing Touches

Complete your character with these final details:

  • Name, age, and apparent age
  • Sire: Who Embraced you?
  • Chronicle Tenets: Finalize with Storyteller
  • Resonance: Your preferred emotional flavor when feeding
  • Relationship Map: Connections to other characters
  • Physical description and history
  • True Age: Time since your Embrace
Experience Points: Some Storytellers grant bonus XP at character creation for experienced chronicles. Discuss this with your group.

The night awaits. May your Requiem be unforgettable.
